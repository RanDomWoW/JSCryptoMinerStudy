The Scoring Process
We looked at five main dimensions in our rating system: customer reviews, durability features, price, health-related features, and warranty. Each category was ranked on a scale of 1-5, with 5 being highest.

 	Consumer Ratings – We analyzed third-party reviews via manufacturer and retailer websites, blog, and other review sources.
 	Price – We compared features versus retail prices. Brands that publish their prices ranked higher, since brands that don’t make their prices public may tend to price gouge, overcharging consumers and subjecting them to high-pressure tactics.
 	Health Benefits – We analyzed the features and consumer reports of health benefits.  	Warranty – We compared warranty length, with greater focus placed on full coverage terms. Twenty-year and even lifetime warranties are fairly common, but critical components like the electrical parts and motors may only have 1-5 years of coverage. This keeps your body in the same place no matter how you choose to position your base, ensuring you can reach everything next to your bed at all times.
 	Modern features like USB ports to charge your phone, a remote stand for easy access to control the bed, and under-bed lighting raise it above some of the more clinical looking brands.

Topping it all off, the Amerisleep Adjustable Bed provides a 20-year limited warranty, which keeps customers adequately covered. The first year covers parts and labor and the next two years cover parts only. The remaining years cover any steel and mechanical base parts found to be defective.
The Other Adjustable Beds of Note
#2) Tempurpedic – 4.1


Tempurpedic features three primary adjustable frames under the Tempur-Ergo name: the Premiere, the Plus and the Grand. The brand came in second, ranking just behind Amerisleep in the comparison. Customer ratings averaged 4.5/5, and Tempurpedic’s offerings also received 4’s for durability and health benefits. Both of the bases have a range of features and performance specifications.

However, points were lost on price for costs higher than average compared to features. What accounts for the heftier price tag? Tempurpedic is well known as a result of their extensive advertising. That kind of publicity costs money, and typically results in higher product prices for customers.

Warranties are fair with the Tempurpedic adjustable bed lines. The Premiere and Grand come with 5-year full coverage and lifetime limited warranties. With the Plus, the warranty is 3-years full coverage with an additional 2-year warranty on parts only. The frame of the Plus has a 25-year limited warranty running concurrently.
#3) Sleep Comfort – 3.8/5


Sleep Comfort ranked third, with pros including good ratings for warranty, health benefits, and durability. On their website, Sleep Comfort offers their brand’s Classic model and the Leggett and Platt brand’s Brio 20 and Prodigy. Their beds received an average of 4.0/5 stars in reviews.

The factor that most affected their ratings was the lack of transparency about the cost of their adjustable bed frames, as they do not publish retail prices. This encourages price gouging, and salespeople may be more likely to take advantage of customers who don't try to negotiate. Sleep Comfort’s lower-end models, the Brio 20 and the Classic B24 also seem limited on features, and warranty details prove vague.
#4) Costco – 3.5/5
 Costco carries two different models: the Leggett and Platt S-Cape and a base from the Sleep Science brand. On average, we found customer ratings to be around 4.0/5  and reporting a favorable level of health benefits, as well as a good range of basic features available in both models.



Warranty terms varied across the models, with some offering more transparency than others. Full coverage periods are 1-year and parts range from 2-5 years. Customers also like Costco’s liberal return policy.

Price, especially on the mattress-adjustable packages, comes in on the high side. However, Costco does publish all of its prices online, making it easy for customers to compare while shopping. One thing to be aware of: If you’re not a Costco member, you’ll pay a 5% surcharge above the listed price.
#5) Golden Rest -2.8/5
Golden Rest offers five lines of adjustable frames with multiple models in each line: Supernal, Companion, Floor Hugger, Night Rider and Valiant. Each line offers several models with various features, so there’s a good amount of flexibility. However, the customer reviews suggest that more choice doesn’t always guarantee a better product.

The adjustable beds scored in average ranges on all aspects we researched, with 3.0/5 in every category. In general, the ratings seem mixed: Many people found health benefits in the beds, but limited longevity and higher prices were seen as high negatives. The look of the bases was also a downside for some buyers, who felt that the appearance looked too similar to something you might find in a hospital.

Golden Rest’s warranty coverage last 1 year for adjustable bases. That’s average for full coverage but far below average for prorated coverage, which typically extends around 20 years.
Recap: Reviews for Adjustable Beds
The biggest influences in our adjustable bed comparison and people’s reviews appear to be transparency and value. Simply put, consumers want to get what they are expecting based on claims made by a brand or by a showroom salesperson. As such, models that offered little information or aren’t fully transparent on their pricing and warranties ranked lower in consumer ratings and in our comparison. (Which is completely understandable, don’t you think?)
Getting the Best Deal
It goes without saying the most people want to get a good price relative to the features and durability of their adjustable bed. And thankfully, online shopping makes this easier than ever by allowing buyers to compare pricing and features for themselves, versus having to rely on salespeople for information. As such, brands that offer stronger values are faring better than brands sticking to outdated models with high markups and high-pressure sales.
Modern Styling


Increasingly, people are also preferring adjustable beds with designs that look like comfortable home furniture, not something you’d find in a hospital. And who can blame them? Many models have a cold, clinical feel that just doesn’t work for most shoppers today.

Unsurprisingly, the top-rated bed frames in our comparison have a more modern look, with upholstered sides and concealed support beams. Most also function within wood frames or with headboards and footboards, helping them blend seamlessly into a customer’s bedroom décor.
Budget Wisely
As with any big purchase, keep your budget front and center when shopping for adjustable bed frames. You can invest in your health without going broke. So pick a number that works for you—and stick to your guns. There are plenty of great deals to be found if you’re willing to comparison shop, and retailers often offer huge discounts near holidays like President’s Day, Memorial Day, and Labor Day. If you don’t see an option in your budget that you like, just wait until the next big sale and take another look.

Also, remember that adjustable beds are often covered by Medicare and other health insurance plans. So it’s worth talking with your provider about getting an adjustable bed and following their instructions to make sure you buy a product that’s covered. Here is what Medicare’s own website says about it:
Medicare will only cover your durable medical equipment (DME) if your doctors and DME suppliers are enrolled in Medicare. Doctors and suppliers have to meet strict standards to enroll and stay enrolled in Medicare. If your doctors or suppliers aren’t enrolled, Medicare won’t pay the claims submitted by them. It’s also important to ask your suppliers if they participate in Medicare before you get DME. If suppliers are participating suppliers, they must accept assignment. If suppliers are enrolled in Medicare but aren’t “participating,” they may choose not to accept assignment. If suppliers don’t accept assignment, there’s no limit on the amount they can charge you.
Finding out if some or all of your adjustable bed costs are covered is a small amount of trouble up front, but it can save you from having to deal with a big headache later on. Find out if your situation will allow for it. If not, don’t be discouraged, there are many adjustables available at a variety of different prices.
Don’t Forget the Most Important Accessory for Adjustable Beds


This review did a deep dive into adjustable bed frames, but it didn’t touch on mattress components. That’s another important thing to factor, since the right mattress can play a big role in contributing to your overall comfort and satisfaction. After all, even the best adjustable bed in the world won’t feel great if you’re sleeping on the wrong mattress!

Most adjustable beds are designed to pair with a mattress of your choosing. (However, a couple of brands do make adjustable beds with their mattresses already attached. Check ahead of time before handing over your credit card so you're not in for a surprise when your new bed frame arrives.) Either way, it’s essential to make sure the mattress you pick works on the adjustable bed model that you ultimately end up purchasing. Some mattresses, like innerspring mattresses, may not bend as you adjust the angles of the bed frame’s head and foot. In general, memory foam models tend to be a better choice. And there's a good chance that they'll offer more of the comfort and support you're looking for.

If you’re on the hunt for a mattress to go with your new adjustable frame, our recommendation is Amerisleep’s AS3. This is the brand’s medium-firm model, and it’s designed to offer support for a range of sleepers. Just like their adjustable bed frames use the latest technology, their mattresses are also revolutionary for the comfort features they include. And if you prefer a firmer or softer feel to your mattress, you can look at another one of their five firmness levels.

Learn more about why the Amerisleep AS3 is our pick for #1 overall mattress.

Of course, it’s best to go with the model that will work for you. If you decide to purchase another option instead, just ask the retailer if your mattress and adjustable bed frame are compatible. If they are, you’re in the clear, and have nights of sound sleeping ahead of you. If not, you'll need to seek out a different mattress that's compatible with your bed frame. Not sure where to find one? The retailer may be able to provide a list of options that will work, so just ask.
Adjustable Bed Frame Brands: Summing It All Up
Yes, there’s a lot to consider before you buy an adjustable bed frame. The good news is that you don’t have to start your search from scratch.

Instead of staring wide-eyed at all the options out there (and there are a lot!) cast your focus on the top adjustable bed brands. According to our research, these models are winners because they offer greater transparency in both policies and costs—so you know what you’re really buying. They also have eye-pleasing designs that mesh with your home and don’t have a hospital feel, and give shoppers the option to choose their own mattress. And of course, they offer higher levels of quality and impressive features for their prices—making them great values.

As you embark on your shopping journey, consider looking into these top choices. Thousands of customers agree that these adjustable bed frames are a great fit. And after taking a look at all that they have to offer, there's a good chance that you'll feel the same way.
