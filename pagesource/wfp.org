WFP’s Centres of Excellence in Brazil and China are among WFP’s key mechanisms for South-South cooperation.

wfp-wrapper pv4"><h2 class="fs4 fs3-lg fs2-xl mb4 tc">Focus areas</h2><div class="mc mw10 mw-none-md"><div class="accordion"><ul class="accordion--list"><li class="accordion--item wfp-grid"><div class="item--header wfp-u-1 wfp-u-md-7-24"><h3 class="item--title wfp-u-1 wfp-u-md-7-24" data-target="item-2887">Climate action <span class="icon-arrow-drop-down-dark"></span></h3></div><div class="item--content wfp-u-1 wfp-u-md-17-24" data-id="item-2887"><div class="item--content-wrap wfp-grid"><div class="content--text wfp-u-1 wfp-u-md-3-5 wfp-u-lg-14-24"><div>
                  Climate change is increasing the frequency of climate-related disasters, creating greater risks of hunger and the breakdown of food systems. The World Food Programme is working with governments, international partners, researchers and local communities to analyse and understand the impacts of climate change. Through programmes, innovations, policy and technical support we are helping those most at risk to become climate resilient and food secure.

<a href="/climate-action">Learn more about climate action</a></div></div><div class="content--img wfp-u-md-2-5 wfp-u-lg-10-24"><div class="img--wrapper" style="background-image:url(http://www1.wfp.org/sites/default/files/styles/page_accordion/public/images/xclimate_thematic_0.jpg,qitok=Lz6odBVW.pagespeed.ic.FBrqs39m9Z.jpg)"></div></div></div></div></li><li class="accordion--item wfp-grid"><div class="item--header wfp-u-1 wfp-u-md-7-24"><h3 class="item--title wfp-u-1 wfp-u-md-7-24" data-target="item-2888">Disaster risk reduction <span class="icon-arrow-drop-down-dark"></span></h3></div><div class="item--content wfp-u-1 wfp-u-md-17-24" data-id="item-2888"><div class="item--content-wrap wfp-grid"><div class="content--text wfp-u-1 wfp-u-md-3-5 wfp-u-lg-14-24"><div>
                  More than 80 percent of the world’s food-insecure people live in countries prone to natural hazards. Disasters disrupt agricultural production and livelihoods, driving poverty and hunger. The World Food Programme works with governments and communities to reduce the impacts of disasters on their food security through resilience building, strengthening the capacities of social safety nets, emergency preparedness, analyses and early warning systems.

<a href="/disaster-risk-reduction">Learn more about disaster risk reduction</a></div></div><div class="content--img wfp-u-md-2-5 wfp-u-lg-10-24"><div class="img--wrapper" style="background-image:url(http://www1.wfp.org/sites/default/files/styles/page_accordion/public/images/xddr_thematic_0.jpg,qitok=K498KSkI.pagespeed.ic.EGxMIVJqBa.jpg)"></div></div></div></div></li><li class="accordion--item wfp-grid"><div class="item--header wfp-u-1 wfp-u-md-7-24"><h3 class="item--title wfp-u-1 wfp-u-md-7-24" data-target="item-2889">Gender equality <span class="icon-arrow-drop-down-dark"></span></h3></div><div class="item--content wfp-u-1 wfp-u-md-17-24" data-id="item-2889"><div class="item--content-wrap wfp-grid"><div class="content--text wfp-u-1 wfp-u-md-3-5 wfp-u-lg-14-24"><div>
                  A sustainable zero hunger world can only be achieved if women, men, girls and boys enjoy equal access to food and nutrition, resources, rights and opportunities. The World Food Programme promotes gender equality through leveraging our food assistance to bridge the gender gap. Gender equality and women’s empowerment are a key aspect of WFP's operations. Our work with school meals, smallholder farmers, health, nutrition and protection programmes boosts access to food and livelihoods.

<a href="/gender-equality">Learn more about gender equality</a></div></div><div class="content--img wfp-u-md-2-5 wfp-u-lg-10-24"><div class="img--wrapper" style="background-image:url(http://www1.wfp.org/sites/default/files/styles/page_accordion/public/images/xgender_thematic.jpg,qitok=pi1avBom.pagespeed.ic.4YJEzo_jPE.jpg)"></div></div></div></div></li><li class="accordion--item wfp-grid"><div class="item--header wfp-u-1 wfp-u-md-7-24"><h3 class="item--title wfp-u-1 wfp-u-md-7-24" data-target="item-2890">Nutrition <span class="icon-arrow-drop-down-dark"></span></h3></div><div class="item--content wfp-u-1 wfp-u-md-17-24" data-id="item-2890"><div class="item--content-wrap wfp-grid"><div class="content--text wfp-u-1 wfp-u-md-3-5 wfp-u-lg-14-24"><div>
                  Chronic malnutrition has profound, long-term effects on health and life prospects. The World Food Programme works with governments and partners to help vulnerable groups, such as women, children, and people receiving treatment for HIV and tuberculosis, access nutritious diets. Our programmes include distributing Specialized Nutritious Foods, fortifying staples, designing and implementing school feeding, and enabling dietary diversification.

<a href="/nutrition">Learn more about nutrition</a></div></div><div class="content--img wfp-u-md-2-5 wfp-u-lg-10-24"><div class="img--wrapper" style="background-image:url(http://www1.wfp.org/sites/default/files/styles/page_accordion/public/images/xnutrition_thematic_0.jpg,qitok=jrvHB-4I.pagespeed.ic.CrqeiLTpq6.jpg)"></div></div></div></div></li><li class="accordion--item wfp-grid"><div class="item--header wfp-u-1 wfp-u-md-7-24"><h3 class="item--title wfp-u-1 wfp-u-md-7-24" data-target="item-2886">Smallholder market support <span class="icon-arrow-drop-down-dark"></span></h3></div><div class="item--content wfp-u-1 wfp-u-md-17-24" data-id="item-2886"><div class="item--content-wrap wfp-grid"><div class="content--text wfp-u-1 wfp-u-md-3-5 wfp-u-lg-14-24"><div>
                  Smallholder farmers are highly vulnerable to hunger. The World Food Programme collaborates with national governments to help forge sustainable food systems more inclusive of smallholder farmers along the value chain. This includes buying their produce for WFP programmes, introducing them to formal markets, and enabling access to skills, knowledge and infrastructure to develop their livelihoods and make them more resilient to risks.

<a href="/smallholder-market-support">Learn more about smallholder market support</a></div></div><div class="content--img wfp-u-md-2-5 wfp-u-lg-10-24"><div class="img--wrapper" style="background-image:url(http://www1.wfp.org/sites/default/files/styles/page_accordion/public/images/xmarketsupport_thematic_1.jpg,qitok=hYjRfvDg.pagespeed.ic.hm9hCwMS6W.jpg)"></div></div></div></div></li><li class="accordion--item wfp-grid"><div class="item--header wfp-u-1 wfp-u-md-7-24"><h3 class="item--title wfp-u-1 wfp-u-md-7-24" data-target="item-2891">Social protection and safety nets <span class="icon-arrow-drop-down-dark"></span></h3></div><div class="item--content wfp-u-1 wfp-u-md-17-24" data-id="item-2891"><div class="item--content-wrap wfp-grid"><div class="content--text wfp-u-1 wfp-u-md-3-5 wfp-u-lg-14-24"><div>
                  National social protection systems can protect citizens from stressors and shocks but, due to inadequate coverage in many developing countries, the poorest remain vulnerable to hunger and poverty. The World Food Programme supports national governments in the design and delivery of nutrition-sensitive social protection programmes and safety nets, such as school feeding programmes, to improve coverage and cost-effectiveness.

<a href="/social-protection-and-safety-nets">Learn more about social protection and safety nets</a></div></div><div class="content--img wfp-u-md-2-5 wfp-u-lg-10-24"><div class="img--wrapper" style="background-image:url(http://www1.wfp.org/sites/default/files/styles/page_accordion/public/images/xsafetynets_thematic_0.jpg,qitok=wig7wFWh.pagespeed.ic.O4ci0tnuOx.jpg)"></div></div></div></div></li><li class="accordion--item wfp-grid"><div class="item--header wfp-u-1 wfp-u-md-7-24"><h3 class="item--title wfp-u-1 wfp-u-md-7-24" data-target="item-2892">Sustainable livelihoods and ecosystems <span class="icon-arrow-drop-down-dark"></span></h3></div><div class="item--content wfp-u-1 wfp-u-md-17-24" data-id="item-2892"><div class="item--content-wrap wfp-grid"><div class="content--text wfp-u-1 wfp-u-md-3-5 wfp-u-lg-14-24"><div>
                  About 80 percent of the world’s poorest and hungriest people live in fragile and degraded environments, prone to recurrent natural disasters. The World Food Programme’s asset creation programmes give vulnerable communities food or cash-based transfers while they build or restore assets that will increase their resilience, enabling them to become food-secure in the long-term.
