        <p><strong>About me:</strong> I am a professor of <a href="http://www.seas.harvard.edu/computer-science">Computer Science</a> at Harvard University, and a member of the Harvard SEAS <a href="http://toc.seas.harvard.edu/">Theory of Computing group</a>. Previously,  I was a principal researcher at Microsoft Research New England, and before that I was an associate professor (with tenure) at Princeton University&rsquo;s Computer
Science department. I hold a Ph.D from the Weizmann Institute of Science, and was a postdoctoral fellow at the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton.</p>

<p><font color="#A41034"><strong>DUS:</strong></font> I am  <strong>co director of undergraduate studies (DUS)</strong>  of Computer Science at Harvard together with  <a href="https://people.seas.harvard.edu/~chong/">Steve Chong</a>. Please email <a href="mailto:cs-dus@seas.harvard.edu">cs-dus@seas.harvard.edu</a> for any DUS related matters.
For information about the Computer Science concentration at  Harvard see the page <a href="http://harvardcs.info"><font color="#A41034"><b>harvardcs.info</b></font></a>. See also <a href="#contact">my contact information and office hours</a>.</p>

<p><strong>Current activities:</strong> Co chair of  the  <a herf="https://windowsontheory.org/2017/10/30/stoc-2018-highlighted-plenary-talks-call-for-nominations/">committee to select invited presentations</a> at the <a href="http://acm-stoc.org/stoc2018/">&ldquo;Theory Fest&rdquo; at STOC 2018</a>. Local co-organizer for the <a href="https://womenintheory.wordpress.com/">Women In Theory Workshop</a> on June 2018 at Harvard. Trustee of the <a href="http://computationalcomplexity.org/">Computational Complexity Foundation</a>. Member of  the editorial board of the
<a href="http://jacm.acm.org/">Journal of the ACM</a>, the <a href="http://theoryofcomputing.org/">Theory of
Computing Journal</a> (ToC) and the <a href="http://eccc.hpi-web.de/">Electronic Colloquium of
Computational Complexity</a>. Member of the <a href="https://thmatters.wordpress.com/catcs/">Committee for the Advancement of Theoretical Computer Science (CATCS)</a>. I am a co organizer of the <a href="http://theory.csail.mit.edu/reading-group">Harvard/MSR/MIT reading group</a>.  See my <a href="cv.pdf"><b>CV</b></a> for past activities.</p>

<p><strong>Current students:</strong>  Zhixian Lei, Yueqi Sheng, <a href="https://cnchou.github.io/">Chi-Ning Chou</a></p>

<p><strong>Current postdoc:</strong> <a href="http://people.eecs.berkeley.edu/~tschramm/">Tselil Schramm</a> (co hosted with Jon Kelner, Ankur Moitra and Pablo Parrilo of MIT)</p>

<p><strong>Former students:</strong> <a href="https://www.cs.bu.edu/~goldbe/">Sharon Goldberg</a>
(co advised with <a href="http://www.cs.princeton.edu/%7Ejrex/">Jennifer Rexford</a>), <a href="http://www.cs.princeton.edu/%7Edxiao/">David Xiao</a> (co advised with <a href="http://www.math.ias.edu/%7Eavi/">Avi Wigderson</a>),
<a href="http://www.cs.virginia.edu/~mohammad/">Mohammad Mahmoody</a>,
<a href="http://mrtz.org/">Moritz Hardt</a>.</p>

<p><strong>Former postdocs:</strong> <a href="http://www.eng.tau.ac.il/~bennyap/">Benny
Applebaum</a>, <a href="http://www.complexity.ethz.ch/people/holthoma">Thomas
Holenstein</a>, <a href="http://research.microsoft.com/en-us/people/guyroth/">Guy
Rothblum</a>.</p>

<p><strong>Former interns:</strong> <a href="http://mrtz.org/">Moritz Hardt</a>, <a href="http://www.cs.berkeley.edu/~jsherman/">Jonah Sherman</a>, <a href="http://www.cs.cmu.edu/~yuanzhou/">Yuan Zhou</a>,  <A href="http://www.cs.columbia.edu/~liyang/">Li-Yang Tan</a> , Aaron Sidford, Aaron Potechin, <a href="http://www.cs.utexas.edu/~kothari/">Pravesh Kothari</a>.</p>

<p><strong>Funding:</strong> I am currently supported by NSF awards CCF 1565264 and CNS 1618026 and a Simons Fellowship. I am grateful for past support by the NSF, as well as the Packard and Sloan foundations and the BSF.</p>

<p><p>I wrote a <strong>textbook</strong> with Sanjeev Arora:  <a href="http://www.cs.princeton.edu/theory/complexity/">Computational
Complexity: A Modern Approach</a>. I also wrote extensive lecture notes on <a href="http://introtcs.org">introduction to theoretical computer science</a> and the <a href="http://sumofsquares.org">sum of squares algorithm</a> (with David Steurer). I occasionally blog on the <a href="http://windowsontheory.org">Windows on Theory blog</a>.</p>

<p><p>See also my <a href="cv.pdf"><font size="+1"><b>curriculum vitae</b></font></a> and <a href="Bio/"><font size="+1"><b>brief bio</b></font></a>.</p>

                <h1>Writing</h1>
                
                  <p><a href="http://www.boazbarak.org/informal/">All writing <i class="fa fa-angle-double-right" style="padding-left: 5px;"></i></a></p> 
            </div>
            <div class="col-xs-12 col-md-8">
                <p>Here are some surveys and essays I wrote, see <a href="http://www.boazbarak.org/informal/">here</a> for more of my non-technical writing. I also occasionally post on the <a href="http://windowsontheory.org">Windows on Theory</a> blog.</p>

<ul>
<li><p><a href="https://eccc.weizmann.ac.il/report/2017/065/">The Complexity of Public-Key Cryptography</a> - survey/tutorial on the computational assumptions landscape of cryptogrpahy. Written in honor of Oded Goldreich&rsquo;s 60th birthday.</p></li>

<li><p><a href="http://www.boazbarak.org/Papers/bayesian.pdf">Bayesianism, frequentism, and the planted clique, or do algorithms believe in unicorns?</a>, April 2016. See also <a href="https://windowsontheory.org/2016/04/13/bayesianism-frequentism-and-the-planted-clique-or-do-algorithms-believe-in-unicorns/">blog post</a> on windows of theory blog and <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nc-ZozzaJ2Y&amp;list=PLYbY2kg6Mm3O0u1uck3NEa_mz_oaRgJXs&amp;index=2">video of a talk at Northwestern</a>.</p></li>

<li><p><a href="http://cacm.acm.org/magazines/2016/3/198855-hopes-fears-and-software-obfuscation/fulltext">Hopes, Fears, and Software Obfuscation</a>, Communications of the ACM, March 2016. See also <a href="Papers/obfuscation-preprint.pdf">preprint version</a> and <a href="https://vimeo.com/154339241">video of short interview</a>.</p></li>

<li><p><a href="http://windowsontheory.org/2016/02/07/speaking-about-unspeakables/">Speaking about  unspeakables</a>, Harvard <a href="http://ltl2016.com/">Lectures that Last 2016</a>, February 2016, see also <a href="https://youtu.be/ZBFowd7-Vrs">video of lecture</a>.</p></li>

<li><p><a href="http://windowsontheory.org/2015/11/03/advice-for-the-budding-theorist/">Advice for the budding theorist</a>, blog post on the Windows on Theory blog, November 2015</p></li>

<li><p><a href="http://eccc.hpi-web.de/report/2014/059/">Sum-of-squares proofs and the quest toward optimal algorithms</a> with David Steurer. Survey, also appeard in proceedings of ICM 2014.
See also <a href="http://www.boazbarak.org/sos">my seminar</a> on this topic, as well as a <a href="https://video.seas.harvard.edu/media/t/1_vxzkcjco">video</a> of a related talk at Harvard.</p></li>

<li><p><a href="http://windowsontheory.org/2013/10/07/structure-vs-combinatorics-in-computational-complexity/">Structure vs Combinatorics in Computational Complexity</a>, Windows on Theory blog, October 2013.  See also <a href="http://eccc.hpi-web.de/report/2013/182/f">adapted version</a> in the bulletin of the European Association for Theoretical Computer Science.</p></li>

<li><p><a href="http://windowsontheory.org/2012/07/31/truth-vs-proof-the-unique-games-conjecture-and-feiges-hypothesis/">Truth vs. Proof - the Unique Games Conjecture and Feige&rsquo;s Hypothesis</a>, Windows on Theory blog, July 2012.  See also <a href="http://www.boazbarak.org/Papers/truth-proof.pdf">adapted version</a>  in logic in computer science column of the bulletin of the European Association for Theoretical Computer Science.</p></li>
</ul>
