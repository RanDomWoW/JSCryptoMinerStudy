<h2>Is Online College Right For Me?</h2>
<hr class="thick-rule">
<p>Despite what some students may think, online colleges are no less challenging than traditional colleges. Depending on a student’s learning style, keeping up with online work may actually prove to be more demanding for some. When asked about the greatest challenges they face while studying online, a majority of students in a <a href="http://www.learninghouse.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/07/OnlineCollegeStudents2015.pdf">Learning House survey</a> cited motivation, attention, and focus as their main concerns.</p>
<p>That being said, many students find the online learning experience to be extremely rewarding. These are some common traits of students who thrive in an online learning environment.</p>

<h2>Paying For Online College</h2>
<hr class="thick-rule">
<p>While accredited online colleges are typically cheaper than their brick-and-mortar counterparts, many online students still need financial help to ease the cost of tuition. Fortunately, an increasing number of online colleges now offer aid for distant learners in the form of scholarships, grants, loans, and occasionally, work-study programs.</p>
<p>The process of applying for student aid at an online college is similar to the application process at a traditional school. To be eligible, students must first fill out the <a href="https://fafsa.ed.gov/">Free Application for Federal Student Aid</a> (FAFSA). The FAFSA becomes available online on October 1 annually; students should start preparing their FAFSA form as soon as possible, as many awards are offered on a first-come-first-serve basis.  Note that students applying for student aid must be enrolled at an online college that has been accredited by a federally recognized accreditation agency.  The status of an online student (full time or part time) may also affect their eligibility to receive aid. </p>
<p>To learn more about paying for online college, review our <a href="http://www.onlinecollege.org/financial-aid-for-online-college/">guide to financial aid for online college</a>, or contact an advisor at your prospective school to discuss financial aid opportunities for distance students.</p>
