<p><strong>Tux Paint</strong> is a free, <a href="/reviews/">award-winning</a> drawing program for children ages 3 to 12 (for example, preschool and K-6).
Tux Paint is used in schools around the world as a computer literacy drawing activity.
It combines an easy-to-use interface, fun sound effects, and an encouraging cartoon mascot who guides children as they use the program.</p>

<p>Kids are presented with a blank canvas and a variety of drawing tools to help them be creative.
(See the <a href="/features/">full list of features</a>.)</p>

<div style="background: #FEE686; margin-left: 10%; margin-right: 200px; padding: 4px; font-size: small;">

<p>Tux Paint runs on a variety of platforms, including all versions of <i>Windows</i> (including Tablet PC), <i>Mac OS X</i> 10.3 and up, <i>Linux</i>, <i>BeOS and Haiku</i>, <i>FreeBSD</i> and <i>NetBSD</i>.
It works well on older, slower systems, as well as thin-clients such as LTSP, Citrix&reg; and Windows Terminal Services.  It even runs on some handheld computers!</p>

<h3>How Much Does It Cost?</h3>

<a href="http://www.tux4kids.com/"><img align=right
src="http://www.newbreedsoftware.com/images/labels/tux4kids_sponsored.png"

<p>Tux Paint is <b>free</b>, Open Source software, distributed under the terms of the GNU <a href="docs/COPYING.txt">General Public License</a>.
It is developed by <a href="developers/">volunteers around the world</a>.  We work on it as a labor of love, with the hopes that people will find it useful, and in turn share it with their own friends and family.</p>

<p>Because Tux Paint (and <a href="http://www.schoolforge.net/education-software" class="external">other open source software</a>) is free of cost and not limited in any way, a school can use it <i>today</i>, without waiting for procurement or a budget!</p>
