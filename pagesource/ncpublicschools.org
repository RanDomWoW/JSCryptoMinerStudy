						<strong><a  href="/newsroom/news/2017-18/20180308-01">Schools to Benefit from  Funds for K-3 Teachers</a></strong><br/>
At State Superintendent Mark Johnson direction, the Department of Public Instruction is providing nearly $5 million to
the state’s school districts and charter schools for additional K-3 literacy support. Every district and charter school with
early elementary grades will receive $200 for each K-3 reading teacher to purchase literacy materials.<br/><br/>

<strong><a  href="/newsroom/news/2017-18/20171205-01">NC Supt. Mark Johnson Announces Redesigned, User-friendly NC School Report Cards Website</a></strong><br/>
School report cards for all public schools in the state are now available with 2016-17 data at <a style=" padding-right:0;" href="https://ncreportcards.ondemand.sas.com/src" target="external"><span style="text-decoration:underline;">NC School Report Cards</span></a>, a completely redesigned, user-friendly website.<br/><br/>

<strong><a href="/docs/accountability/reporting/2017/documentation/exsumm17.pdf">Performance and Growth of NC Public Schools</a> </strong><br />
This report provides growth and performance data for the 2016–17 school year based on analysis of all End-of-Grade and End-of-Course tests in English Language Arts/Reading and Mathematics and the Essential Standards in Science for all district schools and charter schools. <br /><br />

North Carolina's plan to implement the Every Student Succeeds Act has been submitted to the U.S. Department of Education.
