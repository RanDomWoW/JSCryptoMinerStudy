<h2 style="text-align: center">Welcome to Judaism 101!</h2>
Judaism 101 is an online encyclopedia of Judaism, covering Jewish beliefs, 
people, places, things, language, scripture, holidays, practices and customs. 
My goal is to make freely available a wide variety of basic, general 
information about Judaism, written from a traditional perspective in plain 
English. This web site has grown continually for more than 10 years and 
continues to be updated periodically. 
<p>
<a href="alephbet.htm"><img src="graphics/chai.gif" alt="Chai (links to Hebrew Alphabet)" title="Chai (links to Hebrew Alphabet)" class="WideLeft"/></a>
The information in this site is written predominantly from the 
<a href="defs/orthodox.htm" onclick="return ShowDef(this)" onmouseout="hideDef()">Orthodox</a> viewpoint, because I believe that is a 
good starting point for any inquiry into Judaism. As recently as 300 years ago, 
this was the only Judaism, and it still is the only Judaism in many parts of 
the world. Be aware, however, that many Jews do not follow all of the 
traditions described here, or do not follow them in the precise form described 
here. The <a href="defs/conservative.htm" onclick="return ShowDef(this)" onmouseout="hideDef()">Conservative movement</a> believes 
that these laws and traditions can change to suit the times, and 
<a href="defs/movement.htm" onclick="return ShowDef(this)" onmouseout="hideDef()">Reform/Liberal/Progressive movements</a> believe 
that individuals can make choices about what traditions to follow. However, 
what I present here is the starting point, the traditions that are being 
changed or chosen. On some pages, I have identified variations in practice or 
belief in other movements. 
</p>

<h3 id="Start">Where to Start</h3>
<p>
There are over eighty web pages on this site, comprising over 300 pages of 
text, a virtual book of information on Judaism. That's a lot of information! 
Where should you start? That depends on what you're looking for: </p>
<dl>
<dt><b>Just browsing?</b> </dt>
<dd>If you're not sure what you're looking for, and you just want to see what's 
available on this site, look through the <a href="toc.htm">Table of 
Contents</a>. </dd>
<dt><b>Looking for something specific?</b> </dt>
<dd>If you are looking for something specific, I have a 
<a href="search.shtml">Search Engine</a> for this site. Enter a term and it will 
search the glossary and index, giving you quick definitions and explanations, 
and pointing you to appropriate pages in this site. </dd>
<dt><b>Beginner, Intermediate or Advanced?</b> </dt>
<dd><a 
href="torah.htm"><img src="graphics/scroll.gif" class="WideRight" alt="Torah Scroll" title="Torah Scroll"/></a> 
Judaism 101 was originally created as an introduction to Judaism for people 
with little or no knowledge. But like the 
<a href="defs/passover.htm" onclick="return ShowDef(this)" onmouseout="hideDef()">Passover</a> seder, this site now includes 
information for the wise son, who wants to know the ritual details; the simple 
son, who asks simple questions; and the son who does not know what to ask (the 
wicked son can go someplace else &lt;grin&gt;). All pages are now labeled 
appropriately: <br/>
<b style="color: #FF0000"><i>Basic</i></b>: Things 
that every Jewish person should know, that require no prior knowledge<br/> 
<b style="color: #FF0000"><i>Intermediate</i></b>: Beyond the basics<br/> 
<b style="color: #FF0000"><i>Advanced</i></b>: More sophisticated 
concepts<br/> 
<b style="color: #FF0000"><i>Gentile</i></b>: The minimal 
things that Jews expect gentiles to know. There is only one page in this 
category so far (about the holidays) but there may be one or two more in the 
future </dd>
<dt><b>Want to see what's new?</b> </dt>
<dd>If you've been here before and just want to see what's new on the site, try 
the <a href="nu.htm">What's Nu?</a> page, a chronological list of additions and 
significant changes to pages in this site. </dd>
</dl>
