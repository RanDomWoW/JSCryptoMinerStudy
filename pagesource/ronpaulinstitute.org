                                        </a>

									<h4>										
										<span class="author-left">John W. Whitehead </span>
										<span class="date-right">Mar 14, 2018</span>
                                        <br style="clear:both;" />										
									</h4>
									<p>
										<img style="width: 202px; height: 146px;" src="/media/121667/school-lockdown.jpg?width=202px&amp;height=146px" alt="undefined" rel="31832" /><br /><br />Just what we don’t need: <a href="https://www.huffingtonpost.com/entry/doj-school-resource-officers-school-schootings_us_5aa707e3e4b03c9edfaee4eb">more gun-toting, taser-wielding cops</a> in government-run schools that bear an uncomfortable resemblance to prisons.<br /><br />Microcosms of the police state, America’s public schools already contain almost every aspect of the militarized, intolerant, senseless, overcriminalized, legalistic, surveillance-riddled, totalitarian landscape that plagues those of us on the “outside.”<br /><br />Now the Trump Administration wants to double down on these totalitarian echo chambers.<br /><br />The Justice Department, headed by Attorney General Jeff Sessions, has announced that it will <a href="https://www.huffingtonpost.com/entry/doj-school-resource-officers-school-schootings_us_5aa707e3e4b03c9edfaee4eb">provide funding for schools that want to hire more resource officers</a>. The White House has also hinted that it may repeal “Rethink School Discipline” policies, <a href="https://www.usatoday.com/story/news/2018/03/12/devos-commission-eyes-obama-school-discipline-rules/418147002/">heralding a return to zero tolerance policies</a> that treat children like suspects and criminals, especially within the public schools.<br /><br />As for President Trump, he wants to <a href="https://www.politico.com/story/2018/03/01/school-shootings-security-guns-431424">“harden” the schools</a>.<br /><br />What exactly does hardening the schools entail?<br /><br /><a href="https://www.usatoday.com/story/news/2018/03/12/devos-commission-eyes-obama-school-discipline-rules/418147002/">More strident zero tolerance policies</a>, <a href="https://www.huffingtonpost.com/entry/doj-school-resource-officers-school-schootings_us_5aa707e3e4b03c9edfaee4eb">greater numbers of school cops</a>, and all the <a href="https://www.motherjones.com/politics/2018/02/the-nras-plan-to-harden-schools-is-terrifying/">trappings of a prison complex</a>(unsurmountable fences, entrapment areas, no windows or trees, etc.).<br /><br />Just when you thought this administration couldn’t get any more tone-deaf about civil liberties, they prove once again that they have absolutely no regard for the Constitution (especially the Fourth Amendment), no concept of limited government, and no concern for the growing need to protect “we the people” against an overreaching, overbearing police state.

                                        Blaming Russia for Skripal Attack is Similar to ‘Jews Poisoning our Wells’ in Middle Ages
                                    </a>
                                </h2>
                                    <h4>
										<span class="author-left">John Laughland</span>
                                        <span class="date-right">Mar 16, 2018</span>     
                                        <br style="clear:both;" />                               	
                                    </h4>
                                    <p>
                                        <img style="width: 202px; height: 104px;" src="/media/121625/may.jpg?width=202px&amp;height=104px" alt="undefined" rel="31627" /><br /><br />Congratulations to Craig Murray for getting there first. The colorful former British ambassador to Uzbekistan, turned anti-establishment dissident after he was sacked from the Foreign Office in 2004, has published on his <a rel="noopener noreferrer" href="https://www.craigmurray.org.uk/archives/2018/03/the-novichok-story-is-indeed-another-iraqi-wmd-scam/" target="_blank">blog</a> some key texts by authoritative scientists which cast serious doubt on the British government's claims about what happened to the former double agent, Sergei Skripal, and why.<br /><br />Murray – and his sources – have unearthed texts from 2016, 2013 and 1995 by, respectively, a scientist at Porton Down, the secret British military chemical weapons installations which is 20 minutes from Salisbury where Skripal was found last week; a scientist at the Organisation for the Prohibition of Chemical Weapons, the statutory body created by the 1997 Convention on Chemical Weapons but which London has ignored and bypassed in its spat with Moscow; and by the Russian defector scientist, Vil Mirzayanov, who is the sole source for the claim that the Soviet Union started to manufacture so-called novichok ("newbie") nerve agents in the 1980s, allegedly now used to poison Skripal.<br /><br />Two of these texts, for which Murray does not provide links, are available online <a rel="noopener noreferrer" href="https://www.opcw.org/fileadmin/OPCW/CSP/RC-3/en/rc3wp01_e_.pdf" target="_blank">here</a> and <a rel="noopener noreferrer" href="https://www.files.ethz.ch/isn/105521/Report17.pdf" target="_blank">here</a>. The first two show, long before anyone had heard of Sergei Skripal, that the existence of novichoks has not been confirmed. Mirzayanov's 1995 paper says that they could be manufactured anywhere, for instance by any laboratory which can make fertilizer or pesticide, and that the factory where they were allegedly developed by the Soviet Union is in Uzbekistan, a country which has not been under Moscow's control since 1991 but where the Americans had a military base until 2005.
