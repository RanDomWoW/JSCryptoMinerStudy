<h1 class="sectionedit1 page-header" id="welcome_to_the_fieldtrip_wiki">Welcome to the FieldTrip wiki</h1>
<div class="level1">

<p>
FieldTrip is the MATLAB software toolbox for MEG and EEG analysis that is being developed by a team of researchers at the <a href="http://www.ru.nl/donders" class="urlextern" title="http://www.ru.nl/donders" rel="nofollow">Donders Institute for Brain, Cognition and Behaviour</a> in Nijmegen, the Netherlands in close collaboration with <a href="/external_links#collaborating_institutes" class="wikilink1" title="external_links">collaborating institutes</a>. The development of FieldTrip currently receives support from the <a href="http://www.childbrain.eu" class="urlextern" title="http://www.childbrain.eu" rel="nofollow">ChildBrain</a> project and from <a href="http://www.nwo.nl" class="urlextern" title="http://www.nwo.nl" rel="nofollow">the Netherlands Organization for Scientific Research (NWO)</a> and has previously been supported by the <a href="http://humanconnectome.org" class="urlextern" title="http://humanconnectome.org" rel="nofollow">Human Connectome</a> project and <a href="http://www.braingain.nl" class="urlextern" title="http://www.braingain.nl" rel="nofollow">BrainGain</a>. 
</p>

<p>
The toolbox offers advanced analysis methods of MEG, EEG, and invasive electrophysiological data, such as time-frequency analysis, source reconstruction using dipoles, distributed sources and beamformers and non-parametric statistical testing. It supports the <a href="/dataformat" class="wikilink1" title="dataformat">data formats</a> of all major MEG systems (CTF, Elekta/Neuromag, 4D/BTi, Yokogawa) and of most popular EEG systems, and new formats can be added easily. FieldTrip contains high-level functions that you can use to construct your own analysis protocols in MATLAB. Furthermore, it easily allows developers to incorporate low-level algorithms for new EEG/MEG analysis methods.
</p>

<p>
The FieldTrip software is released free of charge as <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open_source" class="urlextern" title="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open_source" rel="nofollow">open source software</a> under the GNU <a href="http://www.gnu.org/copyleft/gpl.html" class="urlextern" title="http://www.gnu.org/copyleft/gpl.html" rel="nofollow">general public license</a>. 
</p>

<p>
This FieldTrip website is a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wiki" class="urlextern" title="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wiki" rel="nofollow">Wiki</a> (just like <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/" class="urlextern" title="http://en.wikipedia.org/" rel="nofollow">Wikipedia</a>), which allows all visitors to contribute, very quickly and easily, without the need for registration. The IP addresses of people that make changes are logged, and they can be blocked if required. Please act responsibly and only make changes that improve this site.
</p>
<div class="noteimportant">Please cite the <strong><a href="http://www.hindawi.com/journals/cin/2011/156869/" class="urlextern" title="http://www.hindawi.com/journals/cin/2011/156869/" rel="nofollow">FieldTrip Reference Paper</a></strong> when you have used FieldTrip for data analysis in your study.
<p>
Robert Oostenveld, Pascal Fries, Eric Maris, and Jan-Mathijs Schoffelen, <strong>“FieldTrip: Open Source Software for Advanced Analysis of MEG, EEG, and Invasive Electrophysiological Data,”</strong> Computational Intelligence and Neuroscience, vol. 2011, Article ID 156869, 9 pages, 2011. doi:10.1155/2011/156869
</p>

</div>

</p>

<h2 class="sectionedit2 page-header" id="news_and_announcements">News and announcements</h2>
<div class="level2">

<p>
<strong> 6 March, 2018 </strong>
</p>

<p>
Springer Nature has updated its policies - Nature journals now ask researchers who submit papers that rely on bespoke software to provide the programs for peer review. More details are in <a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-018-02741-4" class="urlextern" title="https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-018-02741-4" rel="nofollow">this editorial</a>, the <a href="https://www.nature.com/authors/policies/availability.html#code" class="urlextern" title="https://www.nature.com/authors/policies/availability.html#code" rel="nofollow">guidelines to authors</a> have been updated and a <a href="http://www.nature.com/documents/GuidelinesCodePublication.pdf" class="urlextern" title="http://www.nature.com/documents/GuidelinesCodePublication.pdf" rel="nofollow">code and software submission checklist</a> is available. 
</p>

<p>
We would like to invite you to contribute to Frontiers in Neuroscience Special Research Topic <strong>“From raw MEG/EEG to publication: how to perform MEG/EEG group analysis with free academic software”</strong>. The idea is to create a collection of well-described group analyses of EEG and MEG data that can be fully reproduced by anyone and ported by researchers to their own data. Furthermore, as the analyses will be endorsed by peer review, any analysis choices will be citeable in future publications. This will hopefully contribute to wider adoption of good practices by the MEG/EEG research community.
</p>

<p>
See <a href="https://mailman.science.ru.nl/pipermail/fieldtrip/2016-October/011022.html" class="urlextern" title="https://mailman.science.ru.nl/pipermail/fieldtrip/2016-October/011022.html" rel="nofollow">here</a> for the announcement on the FieldTrip mailing list and <a href="http://journal.frontiersin.org/researchtopic/5158/from-raw-megeeg-to-publication-how-to-perform-megeeg-group-analysis-with-free-academic-software" class="urlextern" title="http://journal.frontiersin.org/researchtopic/5158/from-raw-megeeg-to-publication-how-to-perform-megeeg-group-analysis-with-free-academic-software" rel="nofollow">here</a> for the research topic on Frontiers.  
</p>

<strong> 2 October, 2016 </strong>
