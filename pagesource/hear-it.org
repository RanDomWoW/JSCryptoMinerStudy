class="field-item even"><h3>Hearing loss</h3><p><a href="https://www.hear-it.org/hearing-loss">Hearing loss</a> is a natural consequence of getting older. Another common reason for hearing problems is exposure to noise. But hearing loss can also be caused by other factors. Hearing loss can have a range of consequences. The implications differ from person to person, but most people with hearing impairment suffer some social, psychological and physical problems as a result of their hearing loss. In most cases, hearing loss is treated with the use of <a href="https://www.hear-it.org/hearing-aids-1">hearing aids</a>.</p><h3>Tinnitus</h3><p><a href="https://www.hear-it.org/Tinnitus-1">Tinnitus</a> is sounds in the ears or head which do not come from external sound sources. Tinnitus can be experienced as e.g. a hissing or ringing sound. Almost everybody experiences temporary tinnitus, but many have permanent tinnitus which some find very annoying. A connection between tinnitus and hearing loss has been proven and many people with tinnitus often also have a hearing loss.</p><h3>The ear</h3><p>A hearing loss occurs when <a href="https://www.hear-it.org/The-ear---a-magnificent-organ">the ear</a>’s ability to conduct sound from the <a href="https://www.hear-it.org/The-outer-ear">outer ear</a>, through the <a href="https://www.hear-it.org/The-middle-ear-1">middle ear</a> into the <a href="https://www.hear-it.org/The-inner-ear-1">inner ear</a> and further into the brain is damaged or reduced.</p><h3><span style="line-height: 1.2em;">About hear-it.org</span></h3><p>Hear-it.org is a non-commercial web site and has been established to increase public awareness of hearing loss.
