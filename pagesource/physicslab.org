                        <div class="solutionbold" style="text-align: center;">
                            Welcome to the online <a href="All.aspx">physics program</a>, PhysicsLAB&#8482;!
                        </div>
                        <br />
                        PhysicsLAB was started in June of 1997, while I was teaching at <a target="_new" href="http://www.mainlandhighschool.org/">Mainland
                            High School</a> in Daytona Beach, Florida.  As of 2018, the website includes almost 1900 interactive lessons, labs and worksheets. You may examine
                        the <a href="all.aspx">curriculum</a> through any of the links provided on the sidebar.
                        <br />
                        <br />

                        PhysicsLAB is included, under the title <i>Physics Lab Online</i>, in
                        <ul>
                            <li>the American Association of Physics Teachers' <a target="_blank" href="http://www.compadre.org/precollege/search/search.cfm">ComPADRE</a> website 
                        for recommended instructional materials</li>
                            <li>the <a target="_blank" href="https://www.merlot.org/">Merlot II Collection</a> for PhysicsLAB's overall content and special references to the resource lessons, labs, and Amusing Problems</li>
                            <li>the <a target="_blank" href="https://nsdl.oercommons.org/courses/physics-lab-online">National Science Digital Library</a> (NSDL)</li>
                            <li>the Maryland State Department of Education has included the labs in PhysicsLAB in its <a target="_blank" href="https://instructresources.msde.maryland.gov/OIT/">online instructional toolkit</a></li>
                            <li>Cambridge University's Department of Engineering <a target="_blank" href="http://teaching.eng.cam.ac.uk/">online undergraduate program</a></li>

                        </ul>

                        Dolores Gende's <a target="_blank" href="http://dgende.homestead.com/index.html">AP Physics website</a> provides deep links to PhysicsLAB:
                        <ul>
                            <li>as a teaching resource under <a target="_blank" href="http://apphysicsb.homestead.com/teach.html#anchor_9">Physics Lecture Courses</a> as AP Review Sessions,</li>
                            <li>within each content area of the AP Physics B Topic Outline listed in the dropdown list under <a target="_blank" href="http://apphysicsb.homestead.com/tutorial.html#anchor_17">Part II, Index by Topic</a> as Mainland,</li>
                            <li>under <a href="http://apphysicsb.homestead.com/teach.html#anchor_18">Per Physics Educational Research</a> as Next-Time Questions Link 2, and</li>
                            <li>as a source of <a target="_blank" href="http://apphysicsb.homestead.com/ips.html">Interactive Problems</a>.</li>

                        </ul>
                        All of this in addition to the greatest affirmation that lies in the fact that countless teacher-based instructional <a target="_blank" href="/asp/images/PLsources.jpg">websites</a> throughout the nation link to PhysicsLAB to assist their school's students with its lessons, worksheets, and labs.
                        <br />
                        <br />

                        Also available on site are four collections of past state and national tests which have been re-formated into interactive formats.  
                        <ul>
                            <li><a target="_blank" href="/Compilations/NYRegents.aspx">New York Regents Physics</a> exams from 1996 to the present</li>
                            <li><a target="_blank" href="/asp/MCAS/">Introductory Physics</a> exams from the Massachusetts Comprehensive Assessment System (MCAS) from 2004 to the present;</li>
                            <li>North Carolina Physics<a target="_blank" href="/asp/NC/"> Exam Questions</a> from 2004 to the present;</li>
                            <li>numerous <a target="_blank" href="/Compilations/AAPT.aspx">PhysicsBowl Exams and Physics Olympiad Screening Tests</a> sponsored by the American Association of Physics teachers (AAPT) </li>
                        </ul>
                        A compilation of <a target="_blank" href="/asp/PracticeProblems/">random number practice exercies</a> can also be accessed for the sidebar. These worksheets allow students to practice "classic" problems with a different set of values each time they reload the page. Additionally the sidebar provides links to general groupings of interactive <a target="_blank" href="Lessons.aspx">resource lessons</a>, <a target="_blank" href="worksheets.aspx">worksheets</a>, and <a target="_blank" href="labs.aspx">labs</a>.
                        <br />
                        <br />
                        The <a href="TOC.aspx">Table of Contents</a> presents the site's curricular materials in a chapter format, while the <a href="search.aspx">search engines</a> allow you to locate
                        <a href="all.aspx">curriculum</a> by course, curriculum group, topic, skill, and keyword. Any of the original curriculum which was not
                        converted to our new XML format can still be accessed by visiting PhysicsLAB's first edition companion website,
                         <a target="_blank" href="http://online.cctt.org/curriculumguide/">Curriculum Guide,</a> where online materials are organized into content areas and
                        instructional units for visiting educators. Note: this listing is limited to materials created from 1997 until 2003 and is no longer as comprehensive as our current search engines and Table of Contents.                        
                        <br />
                        <br />
                        Collateral math lessons, studyaids, and practice pages that support over 150 algebra,
                        geometry, trig, calculus, and statistics topics needed for the successful completion
                        of high school science courses are available through <a target="_blank" href="http://www.algebralab.org">AlgebraLAB</a>, an two-year project
                        funded by Florida's Enhancing Education Through Technology grants.(<a target="_new"
                            href="http://www.slideshare.net/Mike97/florida-department-of-education">2002-05</a>)<br />
                        <br />
                        PhysicsLAB will continue to be maintained and new curriculum developed each year.  Please take a moment and generalize yourself with the <a href="/Directions.aspx">directions</a> on how to use PhysicsLAB. Although there is no fee to access these materials, my <a href='/directions.aspx#copyright'>usage and copyright</a> requirements are provided on the site's <a href="/Directions.aspx">Directions</a> page.
