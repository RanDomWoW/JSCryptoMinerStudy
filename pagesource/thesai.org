<h1>The Science and Information (SAI) Organization</h1>
<p class="subtitle">The fastest growing research services organization to promote the progress of science; to advance technology; and to inspire global community through events, publications, conferences and technical activities</p>
</article>
<div class="line_2" style="margin:10px 0px 28px;"></div>
<h3 style="font-size:16px;">Recent Interactions</h3>
<div class="block_home_col_1">
<div class="block_topic_news">
<article class="block_topic_post">
<div class="content">
<div class="f_pic"><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=enfZbv-4mjE" class="general_pic_hover play no_fx" data-rel="prettyPhoto" title="John Martinis"><img src="/Content/images/pic_pop_video3.jpg" alt="John Martinis"></a></div>
<p>John Martinis from Google explains how a 7 by 7 array of superconducting qubits can compute over a huge state space of 2^49 = 560 trillion states, which can only be checked using the world’s largest classical supercomputers. He also presented progress towards this "quantum supremacy" experiment.</p>
</div>
</article>
<article class="block_topic_post">
<div class="content">
<div class="f_pic"><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=X6SsJlUPNtw" class="general_pic_hover play no_fx" data-rel="prettyPhoto" title="Valentina Salapura"><img src="/Content/images/pic_pop_video4.jpg" alt="Valentina Salapura"></a></div>
<p>Valentina Salapura is with the IBM Research in the Services Innovation Lab where she is helping IBM realize the value of cloud computing. In 2010, Dr. Salapura served as a lead for the Global Technical Outlook with the IBM Research Strategy and Worldwide Operations team to define IBM’s future research agenda and strategy working with the worldwide IBM research organizations.</p>
</div>
</article>
</div>
</div>
<div class="block_home_col_2">
<div class="block_topic_news">
<article class="block_topic_post">
<div class="content">
<div class="f_pic"><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Kpj4SNGje04" class="general_pic_hover play no_fx" data-rel="prettyPhoto" title="Cognitive Computing"><img src="/Content/images/pic_pop_video1.jpg" alt="Laura Haas"></a></div>
<p>Dr Laura Haas (IBM Fellow and Director of IBM Research’s Accelerated Discovery Lab) shows how Watson, the Jeopardy!-playing computer, an early example of a cognitive system, is growing up, and describe some emerging cognitive applications that may change our world.</p>
</div>
</article>
<article class="block_topic_post">
<div class="content">
<div class="f_pic"><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KyDoFrojEYk" class="general_pic_hover play no_fx" data-rel="prettyPhoto" title="Iris Recognition"><img src="/Content/images/pic_pop_video2.jpg" alt="John Daugman"></a></div>
<p>Prof John Daugman explains how his algorithms achieve the tremendous iris match speed required, and how the critical avoidance of identity collisions (False Matches) is accomplished through the key statistics of encoded pattern entropy.</p>
</div>
