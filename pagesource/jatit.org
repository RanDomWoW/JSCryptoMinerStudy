<td rowspan="2" bgcolor="#e0e0e0" width="66%">
<p class="cmtext" align="justify">          </p>
<div class="cmtext" align="justify"><span class="cmtext">              Journal 
	of Theoretical and Applied Information Technology published since 2005 
	(E-ISSN 1817-3195 / ISSN 1992-8645) is an open access International refereed research 
	publishing journal with a focused aim on promoting and publishing original 
	high quality research dealing with theoretical and scientific aspects in all 
	disciplines of Information Technology. JATIT is an international scientific 
	research journal focusing on issues in information technology research. A 
	large number of manuscript inflows, reflects its popularity and the trust of 
	world's research community. JATIT is indexed with major indexing and 
	abstracting organizations and 
	is published in both electronic and print format. All articles are available 
	under <a href="https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-nc-nd/4.0/">cc by-nc-nd</a> licensing.</span></div>
<div class="cmtext" align="justify"><span class="cmtext"> </span></div>
<p class="cmtext" align="justify"><span class="cmtext">                All 
technical or research papers and research results submitted to JATIT should be 
original in nature, never previously published in any journal or undergoing such 
process across the globe. All the submissions will be peer-reviewed by the panel 
of experts associated with JATIT. Submitted papers should meet the 
internationally accepted criteria and manuscripts should follow the style of the 
journal for the purpose of both reviewing and editing. Indexing information is found at the indexing and abstracting 
page of JATIT.</span></p>
<p class="cmtext" align="justify"><u><b><span class="cmtext">What do we look for 
in your Research while conducting blind Peer Review</span></b></u></p>
<p class="cmtext" align="justify"><b><span class="color_h2"><font size="2">1) 
Relevance</font></span><font size="2"><br>
</font></b><font size="2">Its contents have to be of use to anyone practicing 
one of the disciplines addressed by the journal.</font></p>
<ul>
	<li><font size="2">The paper is relevant to the technical scope of the 
	journal and to the professional interests and activities of its audience.</font></li>
	<li><font size="2">Ideally it should present new knowledge or technology 
	that has the potential to help the reader in their professional work as 
	practicing scientists / engineers.</font></li>
</ul>
<p class="b4more"><b><span class="color_h2"><font size="2">2) Innovation</font></span><font size="2"><br>
</font></b><font size="2">It should present new knowledge or technology, or 
analyze previously known facts in a new way.</font></p>
<ul>
	<li><font size="2">A paper should present new knowledge or analyze 
	previously known facts in a new way. Additionally it should take full 
	account of previously published work on its subject.</font></li>
	<li><font size="2">A case history is appropriate if it presents the 
	application of existing technology in a new way or in a new location or 
	environment where it has not previously been used and that requires new and 
	previously un-used techniques or analyses.</font></li>
	<li><font size="2">A review paper is appropriate if it finds connections 
	between previously unrelated facts or commonality between previously 
	uncompelled facts and results, or makes deductions that give the reader 
	substantiated guidance on the accuracy and applicability of the reviewed 
	analyses.</font></li>
</ul>
<p class="b4more"><b><span class="color_h2"><font size="2">3) Technical Detail</font></span><font size="2"><br>
</font></b><font size="2">It should be logically sound, and it should give 
sufficient detail to allow the reader to replicate the work it describes and to 
assess its applicability to other environments.</font></p>
<ul>
	<li><font size="2">The research design, methods, and analyses are adequately 
	defined and clearly described, well integrated, well reasoned, and 
	appropriate to the aims of the project.</font></li>
	<li><font size="2">The paper should present sufficient detail of the 
	application, methods and analyses employed and results achieved to allow the 
	reader to replicate the work descried but also to evaluate its applicability 
	to the environment and problems on which they are working.</font></li>
	<li><font size="2">Statistical / Analytical tests are appropriate and the 
	assumptions underlying the use of statistics are fulfilled by the data.</font></li>
	<li><font size="2">The statistics are reported correctly and appropriately.</font></li>
</ul>
<p class="b4more"><b><span class="color_h2"><font size="2">4) Presentation and 
Documentation</font></span><font size="2"><br>
</font></b><font size="2">It should present clearly and concisely in the JATIT's 
standard format all relevant data and information to support the conclusions and 
to indicate their limitations.</font></p>
<ul>
	<li><font size="2">The text is well written and easy to follow.</font></li>
	<li><font size="2">The introduction builds a logical case and context for 
	the problem statement.</font></li>
	<li><font size="2">The problem statement is clear and well articulated.</font></li>
	<li><font size="2">The data reported are accurate (e.g., the numbers add up) 
	and appropriate; tables, graphs and figures are used effectively and agree 
	with the text.</font></li>
	<li><font size="2">The amount of data presented is sufficient and 
	appropriate.</font></li>
	<li><font size="2">Results are presented effectively; the interpretations of 
	the results are appropriate and the conclusions accurate.</font></li>
	<li><font size="2">The conclusions are clearly stated and follow from the 
	design, methods, and results; the justification of conclusions is well 
	articulated.</font></li>
	<li><font size="2">The study limitations are discussed.</font></li>
	<li><font size="2">Statistical differences are distinguished from meaningful 
	differences.</font></li>
	<li><font size="2">Practical significance or theoretical implications are 
	discussed.</font></li>
	<li><font size="2">Reference citations are complete, accurate and 
	up-to-date. The number of references is appropriate and their selection is 
	judicious.</font></li>
</ul>
<p class="b4more"><b><span class="color_h2"><font size="2">5) Professional 
Conduct (</b><u><a href="/code_ethics.php">Responsibilty of Authors</a></u><b>)</b></font></span><b><font size="2"><br>
</font></b><font size="2">It must avoid commercialism and plagiarism, and must 
not have been published previously.</font></p>
<ul>
	<li><font size="2">There are no commercial references.</font></li>
	<li><font size="2">There are no instances of plagiarism.</font></li>
	<li><font size="2">Ideas and materials of others are correctly attributed.</font></li>
	<li><font size="2">Substantial portions of the data or study should not have 
	been previously published.</font></li>
</ul>
<p><font size="2">All peer review is now based on the above criterion for all 
submitted manuscripts</font></p>
