        <h2 class="pane-title">
      The King Center    </h2>
    
  <div class="pane-content">
  <p></p><div class="media media-element-container media-full"><div id="file-256" class="file file-image file-image-jpeg">

        <h2 class="element-invisible"><a href="/file/256">kingcenter.JPG</a></h2>
    
  
  <div class="content">
    <img height="147" width="240" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; display: block;" class="media-image  media-image-right media-element file-media-large" typeof="foaf:Image" src="http://www.thekingcenter.org/sites/default/files/styles/large/public/kingcenter.JPG?itok=E2Gpmhtq" alt="" title="" /></div>

  
</div>
</div>The King Library and Archives in Atlanta is the largest repository of primary source materials on Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr. and the American Civil Rights Movement in the world. The collection consists of the papers of Dr. King and those of the organization he co-founded, the Southern Christian Leadership Conference, as well as the records of 8 major civil rights organizations and of several individuals active in the Movement. The archives also include more than 200 oral history interviews with Dr. King’s teachers, friends, family and civil rights associates.
<p> </p>
<h2><a href="plan-your-visit">Plan Your Visit »</a></h2>
<p>The King Center is at the hub of a 23 acre National Historic Site which brings one million visitors each year. Free attractions include the birth home, the crypt of Dr. and Mrs. King, exhibits at Freedom Hall, gift shop/information facility, and reflecting pool. <a href="plan-your-visit">Learn more »</a></p>
