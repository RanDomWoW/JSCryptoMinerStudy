					<h3 class="home"><span style="color: #d00000;"><a style="font-size: 1.17em;" href="https://momath.org/about/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Welcome to the National Museum of Mathematics</a></span></h3>
<p class="contact">The National Museum of Mathematics (MoMath) is an <a href="#AwardWinning">award winning museum</a> that highlights the role of mathematics in illuminating the patterns and structures all around us. Its dynamic <a title="Exhibit Guide" href="https://momath.org/about/exhibit-guide/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">exhibits</a>, <a href="https://momath.org/fieldtrips/composite-gallery/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">gallery</a>, and <a title="Upcoming Events" href="http://events.momath.org" target="_blank" rel="noopener">programs</a> are designed to stimulate inquiry, spark curiosity, and reveal the wonders of mathematics. The Museum’s innovative exhibits will engage folks from 105 to 5 years old &mdash; and even younger!</p>
<p><a href="http://testimonials.momath.org" target="_blank">See what people are saying about MoMath.</a></p>
<p class="contact">The Museum is located at 11 East 26th Street in Manhattan and is open from 10:00 am to 5:00 pm, seven days a week, 364 days a year (MoMath is closed on Thanksgiving Day).</p>
<p><b>Special note:</b> MoMath closes early the first Wednesday of every month, at 2:30 pm.</p>
<p><b>NFL pro John Urschel visited MoMath! <a href="http://urschelvideo.momath.org" target="_blank">urschelvideo.momath.org</a></b></p>
