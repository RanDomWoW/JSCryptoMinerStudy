<a href="/presentations/the-tohoku-effect"><h2 class='fg title'>The Tohoku Effect</h2>
</a><h3 class='presented'>
BY <a href="/users/mark-dytham">MARK DYTHAM</a>
<br>
IN <a href="/cities/tokyo">TOKYO</a>
</h3>
<div class='description ellipsis'>
<p dir="ltr">&ldquo;When the Haiti earthquake struck, we ran an event around the world on 24 hour skype. A Year later, the shoe was on the other foot; the earthquake happened in Japan. 105 cities got together within 3 weeks and we held an Inspire Japan Event.&rdquo;</p>
<p><br />From <a href="http://www.pechakucha.org/cities/tokyo">PKN Tokyo</a>&rsquo;s recent "Powered by PechaKucha: Tohoku 2020: Building a sustainable Post-3/11 Future" Speaker and PechaKucha founder <strong>Mark Dytham</strong> presents "<a href="http://www.pechakucha.org/cities/tokyo/presentations/the-tohoku-effect">The Tohoku Effect</a>" on PechaKucha&rsquo;s and <a href="http://www.pechakucha.org/channels/inspirejapan">PechaKucha Inspire</a>&rsquo;s history. No one could have predicted that the small one-off event would balloon into a popular presentation format to be used the world over. PechaKucha Certainly has its perks; It&rsquo;s free, It&rsquo;s simple, it lets people give their work exposure. Most importantly of all, it builds community. It is such communities that come together when tragedies strike and help build warm spaces.<br /><br />This was "PechaKucha of the Day" on Wednesdaym March 17th, 2016.</p>

<a href="/presentations/when-art-and-science-collide"><h2 class='fg title'>When Art and Science Collide</h2>
</a><h3 class='presented'>
BY <a href="/users/yoko-shimizu">YOKO SHIMIZU</a>
<br>
@ <a href="/events/567735cbbfb6ff3e20000001">VOL 132</a>
ON JAN 27, 2016
</h3>
<div class='description ellipsis'>
<p dir="ltr">&ldquo;I wanted to learn the beautiful mechanism of the universe, nature, and life. So i studied biology and chemistry in my university. And that&rsquo;s when i thought &lsquo;how cool would it be to integrate all of this into art?&rsquo;&rdquo;</p>
<p dir="ltr">In <a href="http://www.pechakucha.org/cities/tokyo/presentations/when-art-and-science-collide">When Art and Science Collide </a>from <a href="http://www.pechakucha.org/cities/tokyo">PechaKucha Night Tokyo</a>&nbsp;Vol. <a href="http://www.pechakucha.org/events/567735cbbfb6ff3e20000001">132</a>, &nbsp;contemporary artist and science researcher <strong>Yoko Shimizu</strong> details her work. One may think that art and Science are total opposites but not Yoko. She finds that the beauty of the scientific principles that surround us to be timeless, limitless, and inspirational. Yoko &ndash;who has won many awards her scientific artworks&ndash; creates installations that integrate both art and science.<br /><br />This was "PechaKucha of the Day" on Friday, February 6th, 2016.&nbsp;</p>
