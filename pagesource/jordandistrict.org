						<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="https://jordandistrict.org/2018/03/west-jordan-high-artists-showcase-chalk-art/" rel="bookmark">West Jordan High Artists Showcase Chalk Art</a></h1>
			
						<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="sep">Posted on </span><a href="https://jordandistrict.org/2018/03/west-jordan-high-artists-showcase-chalk-art/" title="1:39 pm" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date" datetime="2018-03-13T13:39:13+00:00">March 13, 2018</time></a>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
			
					</header><!-- .entry-header -->

				<div class="entry-content">
			<p><a href="https://jordandistrict.org/wp-content/uploads/17C7990.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-65113" src="https://jordandistrict.org/wp-content/uploads/17C7990-300x200.jpg" alt="Chalk Art " width="300" height="200" srcset="https://jordandistrict.org/wp-content/uploads/17C7990-300x200.jpg 300w, https://jordandistrict.org/wp-content/uploads/17C7990-1024x683.jpg 1024w, https://jordandistrict.org/wp-content/uploads/17C7990-450x300.jpg 450w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a>As you walk up to the front entrance at West Jordan High School, you may notice a gallery of art…at your feet!</p>
<p>Long a tradition in Europe, street painting and sidewalk art is thought to originate in Italy in the 16th century. In the style of Italian maddonari or street painters, artists at West Jordan High used their colored chalk to create temporary works of art in front of the school.</p>
<p>While rain will eventually wash away their hard work, the fine artistic ability of the students was on full display for all to see, even if for just a short amount of time.</p>
<p>Here are the ‘best of show’ winners:</p>
<ul>
<li>1st Place: Sirens Call by Maren Walker, Isabelle Contrereas, Katie Kosner, and Chenoah Fuller</li>
<li>2nd Place: Michelle Obama by Britney Perez Vidal, Liliana Rodriguez, and Yasmine Ruelas</li>
<li>3rd Place: When You Wish Upon A Star by Lexie Colbert, Bethanie Crockett. Erin Whitlock, and Katherine Lingwall</li>
<li>Most Creative: &#8220;Untitled&#8221; by Natalie Shade, Jessica Fernandez, and Jared Valero (Octopus and Ship)</li>
<li>Most Original: &#8220;Open&#8221; by Megan Hammer</li>
<li>Teachers Pick: &#8220;Sakura Night&#8221; by Maria Mazzei, Andres Ferrer, and Maria Salazar</li>
</ul>

						<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="sep">Posted on </span><a href="https://jordandistrict.org/2018/03/herriman-high-deca-and-fbla-students-take-first-place-in-shark-tank-event/" title="10:36 am" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date" datetime="2018-03-13T10:36:17+00:00">March 13, 2018</time></a>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

				<div class="entry-content">
			<p style="text-align: left;"><img class="alignright wp-image-65110 size-medium" src="https://jordandistrict.org/wp-content/uploads/IMG_2128-300x225.jpeg" alt="Picture of winning students." width="300" height="225" srcset="https://jordandistrict.org/wp-content/uploads/IMG_2128-300x225.jpeg 300w, https://jordandistrict.org/wp-content/uploads/IMG_2128-1024x768.jpeg 1024w, https://jordandistrict.org/wp-content/uploads/IMG_2128-400x300.jpeg 400w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" />It was a competition based on the television show ‘Shark Tank,’ and some Herriman High School students were in it to win. The DECA and FBLA students created an app called ‘Ask Manny,’ which is essentially a high school dance planning app they prototyped and researched. They presented the app before a panel of judges from Enso Rings, a local company, which actually appeared on the real ‘Shark Tank’ TV show. The three students, Edison Velasco, Peyton Williams and Maelyn Dougher gave it their all presenting to the sharks and walked away with the top prize. They will now present their app in another competition at the University of Utah for a chance to win $20,000. The students also took first place at State DECA and sixth place in State FBLA competition with their ‘Ask Manny’ app. Congratulations to these bright and talented students!</p>

						<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="sep">Posted on </span><a href="https://jordandistrict.org/2018/03/community-comes-together-to-produce-amazing-west-hills-middle-school-musical/" title="10:12 am" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date" datetime="2018-03-12T10:12:14+00:00">March 12, 2018</time></a>			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

				<div class="entry-content">
			<p style="text-align: left;"><img class="alignleft wp-image-65099 size-medium" src="https://jordandistrict.org/wp-content/uploads/DSC_0769-300x188.jpeg" alt="" width="300" height="188" srcset="https://jordandistrict.org/wp-content/uploads/DSC_0769-300x188.jpeg 300w, https://jordandistrict.org/wp-content/uploads/DSC_0769-1024x640.jpeg 1024w, https://jordandistrict.org/wp-content/uploads/DSC_0769-480x300.jpeg 480w, https://jordandistrict.org/wp-content/uploads/DSC_0769.jpeg 1280w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" />It is being called a magical experience made possible by members of the community who have given countless hours to make sure students at West Hills Middle School have a chance to perform in a musical. More than 50 PTSA volunteers are behind the school’s production of ‘Singin in the Rain.’ The musical involves more than 120 students in the cast and crew. Parents say it has changed lives and has had a positive impact on those who might be struggling or never thought they would have this opportunity. The community volunteers made and paid for all the props, costumes and set, which they designed and built. It has become such a huge hit that every single show is sold out in the auditorium, which seats 640 people. Congratulations to everyone involved and thank you to the dedicated PTSA volunteers who made the play possible.</p>
