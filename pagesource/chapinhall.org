<h2><a href="/news/articles/university-and-chapin-hall-announce-2017-18-joint-research-fund-awards">University of Chicago and Chapin Hall Announce 2017-18 Joint Research Fund Awards</a></h2>

<div class="row-body">
  <div class="views-field-body">
    <p>The University of Chicago and Chapin Hall have announced $200,000 in awards from the UChicago-Chapin Hall Joint Research Fund to three research teams. This is the fifth year of the Fund.
Awards aim to foster long-term policy-relevant research collaborations between the University and Chapin Hall, while supporting outstanding work that leads to new directions in policy and practice aligned with Chapin Hall’s mission to promote the well-being of vulnerable children, youth and families. Over the past four years, eleven research teams have received awards. <a href="/news/articles/university-and-chapin-hall-announce-2017-18-joint-research-fund-awards" class="bold-more-link">&raquo;</a></p>  </div>

<h2><a href="/voyc">One in Ten Young Adults Experience Homelessness During One Year</a></h2>

<div class="row-body">
  <div class="views-field-body">
    <p>A <a href="http://voicesofyouthcount.org/brief/national-estimates-of-youth-homelessness/">groundbreaking new study</a> released today by Chapin Hall at the University of Chicago reveals one in 10 young adults between the ages of 18 and 25, and at least one in 30 adolescents between the ages of 13 and 17, experience some form of homelessness over the course of a year. This study, also published in the <a href="https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jadohealth.2017.10.006" target="_blank"><i>Journal of Adolescent Health</i></a>, captures youth homelessness broadly, including situations such as sleeping on the streets, in shelters, running away, and couch surfing. <i>Missed Opportunities: Youth Homelessness in America</i> demonstrates the diverse circumstances of young people who experience homelessness, identifies who is most at risk, and illuminates a nationwide problem cutting across rural and urban areas alike. <a href="/voyc" class="bold-more-link">&raquo;</a></p>  </div>
</div>

<h2><a href="/news/articles/qualitative-study-becoming-man-bam-program">Qualitative Study on the Becoming a Man (BAM) Program</a></h2>

<div class="row-body">
  <div class="views-field-body">
    <p>Senior Researcher Jiffy Lansing and Project Associate Erin Rapoport have released a qualitative study on the Becoming a Man Program. The study explores how youth development occurs in the context of BAM programming and how this is likely related to positive outcomes for these youth. The report documents BAM's key program components from the perspectives of participating youth and BAM counselors. <a href="/news/articles/qualitative-study-becoming-man-bam-program" class="bold-more-link">&raquo;</a></p>  </div>
</div>

<h2><a href="/news/articles/research-fellow-amy-dworsky-coauthors-nccp-policy-brief">Research Fellow Amy Dworsky Coauthors NCCP Policy Brief</a></h2>

<div class="row-body">
  <div class="views-field-body">
    <p>Chapin Hall Research Fellow Amy Dworsky coauthored a recently released <a href="http://stage.nccp.org/publications/pub_1165.html">policy brief</a>, “Fostering Health: The Affordable Care Act, Medicaid, and Youth Transitioning from Foster Care.” The brief was published by the National Center for Children in Poverty. It describes the health care needs of former foster youth and the barriers they face to accessing health care. It also makes policy and practice recommendations to eliminate those barriers such as informing youth about their healthcare eligibility and streamlining the application process. <a href="/news/articles/research-fellow-amy-dworsky-coauthors-nccp-policy-brief" class="bold-more-link">&raquo;</a></p>  </div>
