    <h2 class="section_heading"><span>Manifesto</span></h2>

  <div class="modal-body">
  <p><img src="https://d3n8a8pro7vhmx.cloudfront.net/volt/pages/62/attachments/original/1517648098/menifesto01.png?1517648098" alt="" /> <img src="https://d3n8a8pro7vhmx.cloudfront.net/volt/pages/62/attachments/original/1517648099/menifesto02.png?1517648099" alt="" /> <img src="https://d3n8a8pro7vhmx.cloudfront.net/volt/pages/62/attachments/original/1517648099/menifesto03.png?1517648099" alt="" /></p>
<p class="bold"> </p>
<p class="bold"><em>Serve our citizens • Create opportunities • Overcome injustices</em></p>
<h2>Status of European Societies</h2>
<p> </p>
<p>European societies of the 21st century face many social, political, and economic challenges.</p>
<p>On the social level, many <strong>citizens are excluded from partaking in society</strong> and living a decent life. Our system neglects certain disadvantaged groups and regions. The pillars of our social system – the foundation of our societies – are becoming less and less sustainable. Immigration and integration require a re-thinking of our national and European identities and stretch our idea of solidarity.</p>
<p>National politics seem to be <strong>stuck in old divisions</strong> of left versus right and liberal versus conservative, but fail to give answers in an insecure and fast-changing world. This perceived insecurity creates a room for extremist movements and parties, who offer ready-made and simplistic answers to questions of security, identity, and solidarity. The European Union, the political and social project of our grandparents and parents, <strong>is blocked</strong> by quarrels of competence<strong> </strong>and failure to align on shared interests.</p>
<p>Our national economies face <strong>low growth rates</strong>, derailed financial sectors and lack the conditions to nurture innovation. Economic crises yield detrimental effects on our daily lives. <strong>Diverging economies become a threat</strong> to European cohesion. Digitization and automation of labour threaten to make large parts of our workforce obsolete, while globalized markets exert pressure on our wage levels. Markets become more and more influential in shaping many spheres of society, ranging from education to the arts and sciences.</p>
<h2>The Movement</h2>
<p> </p>
<p>Volt is a pan-European progressive movement.</p>
<p><strong>(1) Pan-European</strong>, because we strongly believe that in addition to change at the national level, we need a strong and united Europe to confront the challenges described.</p>
<p><strong>(2) Progressive</strong>, because we promote new answers to build a better future, rather than chasing mistaken images of a long gone past.</p>
<p><strong>(3) A movement,</strong> because it is time to unite and become a strong voice to promote our beliefs.</p>
<p>Volt strongly believes that European states and Europe as a whole can overcome the current <strong>social, political, and economic challenges</strong>; and that our old continent can become an area of highly functioning, livable, and enlightened societies. Volt strongly believes in democratic decision making regarding its own political agenda, not along the lines of classical party doctrines.</p>
<h2>The Individual, the State and the Economy</h2>
<p> </p>
<h3>The rights of the Individual</h3>
<p>We believe in the right of individuals to <strong>choose how they want to lead their life</strong>. This includes the rights to choose one’s religion, sex, sexual orientation, secondary education, profession, and personal goals. Furthermore, we believe that individuals should have the <strong>right and duty to contribute</strong> to the development of their own community, to mold a more efficient and inclusive society.</p>
<h3>The role of the State</h3>
<p>We believe that the State is the <strong>guarantor</strong> for the rights of every individual, the <strong>enabler</strong> for the young and for vulnerable individuals to partake fully in society, and the <strong>system of solidarity</strong> by which we ensure a minimum decent standard of living for everyone. No one should be left behind. As a mean, rather than an end in itself, we believe the State should intervene as little and as fast as possible, and as much and as long as needed. We believe that the government should regularly and democratically decide on its way forward and constantly evaluate the State’s effectiveness in all its dimensions.</p>
<h3>The free and fair economy</h3>
<p>We believe that a <strong>free and open market economy</strong>, within the rules of a functioning legal system and with equal chances to participate and thrive, creates the greatest possible wealth for everyone. We believe that the state cannot plan or foresee innovations, but that it needs to <strong>create a space for innovation</strong> to take place and enable research. We believe that the market economy creates <strong>systemic imbalances, which need to be counterweighted</strong>. We believe that the agglomeration of additional wealth should be possible for the hard working. We believe that the right to a decent life includes leisure. We believe that we need to value all professions, especially those that benefit our societies the most – for example those in education and care.</p>
<h2>Definition of success</h2>
<p> </p>
<p>Our long-term vision is enshrined in three fundamentals goals:</p>
<h3>(1) Maximization of prosperity</h3>
<p>The maximization of the economic prosperity of European nations and their citizens in a sustainable manner.</p>
<h3>(2) Elimination of prejudice and barriers</h3>

<h3>(2) Efficiency</h3>
<p>We express the need to make the best use of our human, natural, and economic resources, in order to fight waste and value the resources given to us. We put a strong emphasis on moving towards a stewardship approach concerning our natural resources.</p>

<p>We express our conviction that every citizen should face the same opportunities when it comes to personal development and success, independent of age, region, race, sex, sexual orientation, and religion.
