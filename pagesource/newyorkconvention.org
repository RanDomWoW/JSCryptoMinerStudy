						<h1>The New York Convention</h1>
					   <div class="Text">
						   <p>The Convention on the Recognition and Enforcement of Foreign Arbitral Awards, also known as the "New York Arbitration Convention" or the "New York Convention", is one of the key instruments in international arbitration. The New York Convention applies to the&nbsp;recognition and enforcement of foreign arbitral awards&nbsp;and the&nbsp;referral by a court to arbitration.</p>
<p>The&nbsp;<a href="/">www.newyorkconvention.org</a>&nbsp;website gives access to&nbsp;information regarding the New York Convention in general, its history, its intepretation and&nbsp;application by the courts, a bibliography, and other relevant matters such as a "draft" for a revised Convention.&nbsp;</p>
<p>This website also provides you with easy-to-use tools to find case law on&nbsp;how the courts have interpreted and applied the New York Convention's provisions&nbsp;(more than 1750 court decisions from more than 65 countries).&nbsp; The&nbsp;tools consist of Consolidated Lists and a Commentary. &nbsp;This information is provided in association with the&nbsp;<a title="The International Council for Commercial Arbitration" href="http://www.arbitration-icca.org/index.html" target="_blank">International Council for Commercial Arbitration</a>&nbsp;and <a title="Kluwer Arbitration" href="http://www.kluwerarbitration.com/" target="_blank">kluwerarbitration.com.</a></p>
<p>The&nbsp;login&nbsp;is presently reserved for courses on the New York Convention, including the course on International Arbitration and the New York Convention at Tsinghua University School of Law in Beijing; the LL.M. in International Dispute Settlement (MIDS) in Geneva; and the "<a title="New York Convention Roadshow" href="http://www.arbitration-icca.org/NY_Convention_Roadshow.html" target="_blank">New York Convention Road Show</a>"&nbsp;for the judiciary in various Contracting States (most recently in Indonesia).</p>
