  <div class="announce-title-text">        <a href="/admin/que_pasa/sunnyside-bioscience-students-awarded-conservation-internships">SUNNYSIDE BIOSCIENCE STUDENTS AWARDED CONSERVATION...</a>  </div>  
  <div class="announce-body-text">          </div>  
  <div class="announce-body-text">        Twelve students from&nbsp;Steven Uyeda's&nbsp;Bioscience program at Sunnyside High School were named Earth Conservation Interns and were recognized by Tucson Mayor&nbsp;Jonathan Rothschild&nbsp;and Pima County District 5 Supervisor&nbsp;Richard Elias&nbsp;at a public event at Kennedy Park on Saturday, March 10th.&nbsp;As part of their paid internship, students will work for five days on priority restoration projects on the Coronado National Forest.



This year's Earth Conservation interns are:&nbsp;Krisna Lopez, Sophia Garcia, Alyssa Vildusea, Noah Rios, Angelica Pichardo, Jordan Moreno, Luis Gustavo Rosas, Mya Phelps Ulloa, Alexa flores, Noe Carrasco, Sharon Vega,&nbsp;and&nbsp;Veronica Portillo.



The conservation internship introduces students to public lands, conservation issues, natural resource careers and ways to live more sustainably. Students who participate in this program will have the opportunity to take coursework at Pima Community College as part of a $5.4 million Youth Career Connect program supported by the U.S. Department of Labor through June 2018.&nbsp;

  <div class="announce-title-text">        <a href="/admin/que_pasa/sunnyside-and-desert-view-students-continue-cte-legacy">Sunnyside and Desert View students continue CTE legacy</a>  </div>  
  <div class="announce-body-text">          </div>  
  <div class="announce-body-text">        Students from Sunnyside and Desert View High School signed their letters of intent&nbsp;to enroll in the Automotive Technology, Business, and Machine Tool Technology programs at Pima Community College (PCC) last week. The event was part of CTE National Letter of Intent Signing Day.&nbsp;

Sunnyside Unified School District's Automotive and Precision Manufacturing programs are highly recognized for their rigor and pathways to certification. How deep is the CTE bench at SUSD?&nbsp;Currently, Desert View High School's Precision Manufacturing program produces parts for Boeing. And last year, nine students from Sunnyside's Automotive program earned MOPAR&nbsp;level zero certification, which is the first in a series of trainings for the next generation of Chrysler, Jeep, Dodge, Ram, SRT and FIAT technicians.&nbsp;

The event, which took place on February 15th at PCC West Campus,&nbsp;was presided by Pima Community College Chancellor, Dr. Lee Lambert, and attended by SUSD Governing Board President, Buck Crouch; instructors Art Basurto and Nubia Moreno, and Sunnyside High School Freshman Academy Assistant Principal, Ricky Torres.&nbsp;

  <div class="announce-title-text">        <a href="/admin/que_pasa/azmerit-scorecard-see-how-we-compare">AzMerit Scorecard: See how we compare</a>  </div>  
  <div class="announce-body-text">          </div>  
  <div class="announce-body-text">        The results from Sunnyside’s 2017 AzMERIT scorecard show significant growth in the District’s aggregate math scores. Sunnyside was the only district in Southern Arizona that posted significant increases in math scores from 2016 to 2017 (+7%); and double digit increases from 2015 to 2017 (11%) when compared to TUSD, Amphi, Flowing Wells and Vail.

Last year, Sunnyside implemented new math curriculum throughout the district; the bump in test results suggest the District is starting to see early signs of the curriculum taking effect. Whereas other area school districts are showing single digit gains throughout the last three testing years, Sunnyside’s scores suggest that not only the District is experiencing improvement, but that improvement is coming at a rate faster than its peer districts despite flat aggregate English Language Arts (ELA) growth between last year and this year.
