	      <a href="http://fixnation.org/2017/07/4th-july-fireworks-cat/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to 4th of July Fireworks and Your Cat">
	<img src="http://fixnation.org/wordpress/wp-content/themes/arthemia/scripts/timthumb.php?src=/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/fireworks.jpeg&w=150&h=150&zc=1&q=100"
alt="4th of July Fireworks and Your Cat" class="left" width="150px" height="150px"  /></a>
      
	<p>Independence Day is fast approaching, a holiday that many Americans love and most cats detest. The sound of fireworks can be very frightening for animals, particularly cats with their highly sensitive ears. Here are some tips to make the 4th less stressful for everyone:</p>
<p>Keep your cat inside the evening of the fireworks. Check windows and flap doors to ensure they are securely closed.<br />
If your cat isn’t microchipped, make sure he is wearing a collar with ID tags, just in case your cat escapes, gets scared and runs away.<br />
