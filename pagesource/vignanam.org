āyurvittaṃ gṛhacchidraṃ mantramauṣadhasaṅgamau |<br/>
dānamānāvamānāśca nava gopyā manīṣibhiḥ ||<br/>
<div class=slokaattribution1 align=center>

<td class=slokameaning>
A wise person keeps these nine things only to one's self as secrets, including one's age, how much money one has, house matters, sacred sayings, making of medicinal drugs, one's private life, how much money one gave to charity and his humiliation.
<br/>
<div class=slokaattribution2 align=center>
- excerpt from <b><a href="http://www.vignanam.org/">Unknown</a></b> <br/>
- by <a href=AUTHORLINK>Unknown</a>

<td width=450 class=slokatext>
śoko nāśayate dhairyaṃ śoko nāśayate śrutam |<br/>
śoko nāśayate sarvaṃ nāsti śokosamo ripuḥ ||<br/>
<div class=slokaattribution1 align=center>
- <b><a href="http://www.vignanam.org/">vālmīki rāmāyaṇam (2.62.15)<br/></a></b> <br/>
- <a href=AUTHORLINK>vālmīki<br/></a> <br/>
</div>
</td>
<td class=slokameaning>
Grief destroys one’s courage. It destroys one’s learning. It destroys one’s everything. There is no enemy greater than grief.
[Context: These are the words of Kausalya. She had spoken harsh words to Dasratha for sending Rama to the forests. She repents and says that she spoke thus because of her intense grief on Rama being sent to the forests]
<br/>
<div class=slokaattribution2 align=center>
- excerpt from <b><a href="http://www.vignanam.org/">Valmiki Ramayanam (2.62.15)</a></b> <br/>
- by <a href=AUTHORLINK>Valmiki</a>
</div>

<div class=slokaattribution1 align=center>
- <b><a href="http://www.vignanam.org/">vālmīki rāmāyaṇam (6.101.14)<br/></a></b> <br/>
- <a href=AUTHORLINK>vālmīki<br/></a> <br/>

<td class=slokameaning>
One may have wives and relatives in every country but one cannot have a brother like Lakshmana in every place.
[Context: Rama’s words of sorrow when Lakshmana becomes unconscious in the battle field struck by the spear of Indrajit.]

<div class=slokaattribution2 align=center>

<td width=450 class=slokatext>
śrīman kṛpājalanidhe kṛtasarvaloka
sarvaṅña śakta natavatsala sarvaśeṣin |
svāmin suśīla sulabhāśrita pārijāta
śrīveṅkaṭeśacaraṇau śaraṇaṃ prapadye ||
<div class=slokaattribution1 align=center>
- <b><a href="http://www.vignanam.org/veda/venkateswara-prapatti-english.html">śrī veṅkaṭeśvara prapatti</a></b> <br/>
- <a href=AUTHORLINK>prativādhi bayaṅkaram anna vedāntācāri</a> <br/>
</div>
</td>
<td class=slokameaning>
Oh Lord of maha Lakshmi! You are an ocean of mercy. You pervade
all universes. You are the witness to all things happenings
and You are aware of everything. You are omnipotent.
You are the ultimate and residual entity of everything.
You come to the rescue
of those, who call out to You in their states of utter helplessness.
You serve as the boon-granting PaarijAthA tree to those,
who surrender themselves at Your sacred feet.
<br/>
<div class=slokaattribution2 align=center>
- excerpt from <b><a href="http://www.vignanam.org/veda/venkateswara-prapatti-english.html">Sree Venkateswara Prapatti</a></b> <br/>
- by <a href=AUTHORLINK>Prathivadhi Bayankaram Anna Vedanthachari</a>
</div>
</td>
</tr>

<td width=550 class=slokatext>
satyaṃ vada | dharmaṃ cara | satyānna pramaditavyam |
dharmānna pramaditavyam | kuśalānna pramaditavyam |
bhūtyai na pramaditavyam | svādhyāyapravacanābhyāṃ na pramaditavyam |
deva pitṛkāryābhyāṃ na pramaditavyam |
mātṛdevo bhava | pitṛdevo bhava | atithidevo bhava | ācāryadevo bhava |
<div class=slokaattribution1 align=center>
- <b><a href="">śīkṣāvalli, taittirīya upaniṣad</a></b> <br/>
- <a href=AUTHORLINK>kṛṣṇa yajurvedaḥ</a> <br/>
</div>
</td>
<td class=slokameaning>
Always speak the truth and follow the dharma (righteousness).
Hold on to truth and dharma. Hold on to welfare activities.
Acquire wealth legitimately. Hold on to self-study and teaching.
Hold on to the worship and rituals of God, parents and ancestors.
Look upon your Mother, Father, Teacher and Guest as living
Gods.
<br/>
<div class=slokaattribution2 align=center>
- excerpt from <b><a href="">Sheeksha Valli, Taittiriya Upanishad</a></b> <br/>
- by <a href=AUTHORLINK>Krishna Yajur Veda</a>
</div>
