				<h2><a href="https://www.venganza.org/2017/08/noodle-mass-germany/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Noodle Mass discrimination in Germany">Noodle Mass discrimination in Germany</a></h2>
				<div class = "titlewrapbyline">Published August 7th, 2017  by Bobby Henderson </div>
        	</div><!--.titleandpublished-->

            <div style="clear:both;"></div><!--#cleardiv-->                                          
</div><!-- .titlewrap --> 

                
           



                
                <div class="entry">
                
                			                      
					<p>German courts have ruled that Pastafarians are not allowed to post announcements of services alongside those of other churches. </p>
<p>One of the signs that was ordered to be removed: </p>
<p><img src="https://www.venganza.org/wp-content/uploads/2017/08/nudelmessesign.jpg" alt="" width="631" height="436" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-7020" srcset="https://www.venganza.org/wp-content/uploads/2017/08/nudelmessesign.jpg 631w, https://www.venganza.org/wp-content/uploads/2017/08/nudelmessesign-300x207.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 631px) 100vw, 631px" /></p>
<p>The <a href="http://www.pastafari.eu">German Pastafarians</a> put these signs up years ago in Templin after other churches were allowed to post signs.  There were complaints by locals who felt mocked, and it&#8217;s been in the Brandenburg state court system ever since. </p>
<p>It&#8217;s disappointing that the courts would discriminate against the Church of the Flying Spaghetti Monster.</p>
<p>From the <a href="http://nypost.com/2017/08/03/spaghetti-monster-church-cant-advertise-its-noodle-masses-in-germany/">New York Post</a>, </p>
<blockquote><p>Brandenburg state court ruled Wednesday the group can’t claim the rights of a religious or philosophical community. Judges said its criticism of others’ beliefs doesn’t constitute a philosophy.</p></blockquote>
<p>I find the reasoning dishonest.  They could have made a fair point to say Pastafarians do not share an unquestioning faith of our own Dogma in the way that happens in many religions &#8211; that we do not necessarily Truly Believe our outlandish stories in the way that others might &#8211; but it is an absurd idea that the majority of Pastafarians have joined the Church in order to criticize others&#8217; beliefs.  What a cynical view.  </p>
<p>Is it hard to believe that people who are wired to reject faith could be drawn to a religion that respects Reason, one that is OK with Believers not fully swallowing Dogma?  Perhaps there are people who enjoy the community of a church, without the necessity of denying Reality? </p>
<p>Aside from that, I do not feel that Pastafarians are inherently judgmental people &#8212; I haven&#8217;t yet experienced this &#8220;criticism of others&#8217; beliefs&#8221; that the courts have projected onto our members.  In fact that sort of judgmental criticism sounds like what I&#8217;ve seen in mainstream churches. </p>
<p>No matter.  I&#8217;m confident that we&#8217;ll win in the end.  And I think there is value when government institutions are forced to show their biases.  They have ruled against us, but in doing so have exposed their own discriminatory attitude.  It may be that the growth of the Church of the FSM is in part due to the reaction of fair-minded people, after these setbacks happen. In other words, a few small-minded people with power have asserted their Authority and &#8220;won&#8221;, but many more people will hear of what took place and form their own opinions, and that is a win for us. </p>
<p>I would like to thank the <a href="http://www.pastafari.eu">German Pastafarians</a> for keeping up the fight.  You are an inspiration to us all. </p>
<p>The best article about this court case is <a href="http://www.dw.com/de/das-gericht-und-das-fliegende-spaghettimonster/a-39913023">here</a>.  At the top right you can translate into English. </p>

<p><strong>Big news</strong></p>
<p>The government of Australia, after years of court proceedings, has decided that Pastafarians will be allowed to wear religious headgear in ID photos.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Here&#8217;s the backstory</strong></p>
<p>A few years ago, a member of the <a href="https://pastafarians.org.au">Church of the Flying Spaghetti Monster Australia</a> (CFSMA), was allowed to wear his headgear (colander) when photographed for his license, but later forced to <a href="https://pastafarians.org.au/media-releases/media-release09032015/">surrender the license for a replacement</a>, without headgear.</p>
<blockquote><p>Preshalin obtained his NSW driver’s license last year, and, at the time, was permitted to wear his religious headwear for his photograph. <p>The CFSMA has been fighting in court ever since, and today they were informed that a decision had been made to allow Pastafarians equal religious protection. Victory!</p>
<p>From Captain Tanya Watkin&#8217;s <a href="https://pastafarians.org.au/media-releases/letter-from-minister-pavey-re-nsw-drivers-license-22-may-2017/">media release</a>:</p>
<blockquote><p>On behalf of the CFSMA I am delighted to announce that the Hon. The new policy will reflect the fact that NSW Government Departments will show respect and consideration towards all people who wear a head covering. When the new policy is implemented, people will no longer have their stated religion checked against a list of ‘recognised’ religions.</p></blockquote>
<p><strong>Congratulations to the CFSMA</strong></p>
<p>The Church of FSM Australia continues to blow me away. Tanya Watkins, Captain of CFSMA, especially, deserves a round of applause &#8212; she took it on herself to lead the fight, and after more than two years, won a victory for Pastafarians everywhere.</p>
<p>Sincere thanks, and congratulations, to Tanya and everyone from the CFSMA.</p>

<p>President Trump:  you should build a monument instead of a border wall. </p>
<p>I won&#8217;t make the usual arguments against the wall &#8212; that border apprehensions are at a 40-year low; that immigrants are not a leading (or growing) cause of crime; that it is technology, not immigrants, taking American jobs. <p>But I&#8217;m sure you understand this. </p>
<p>The wall clearly is about symbolism, about the message it sends to the world.  And about you, and what your presidency represents.  Here is a wall, you people stay on your side &#8211; America is full. The wall is about drawing a line, about Real American values, about American toughness.</p>

<p>Let me next appeal to your sense of aesthetics. You&#8217;ve spent your life building magnificent buildings, beautiful golf courses, resorts and so on.  The Wall will be ugly and plain, the money must be spread across too many hundreds of miles to be spectacular in any given spot.  No one will want to come to look at it, no one will be impressed with it&#8217;s grandeur.  Do you want that to be your legacy?</p>
<p>So I propose you announce a different project &#8212; a grand, glittering monument &#8212; one that promotes the idea of American Toughness, and which &#8211; I think economists will back me up &#8211; is a better use of our tax dollars.  </p>
<p>Rather than describe the monument in words, I&#8217;ve commissioned an architectural concept drawing of the monument:</p>

                <div class="entry">
                
                			                      
					<p>Marcus Bowring out of Victoria, Australia, was successful in getting his Driver&#8217;s license photo taken while wearing Pastafarian religious headgear.  </p>
<p><img src="https://www.venganza.org/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/marcusID.jpg" alt="marcusID" width="620" height="348" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6458" srcset="https://www.venganza.org/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/marcusID.jpg 620w, https://www.venganza.org/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/marcusID-300x168.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 620px) 100vw, 620px" /></p>
<blockquote><p>&#8220;I kept the strainer in a bag until it was time to take the photo because I was bit worried that there were a few bigots around that might not accept it,&#8221; he said.</p>
<p>&#8220;But the young guy that was taking the photo was quite happy. He said something along the lines of &#8216;respect&#8217; or &#8216;well done&#8217;. He was perfectly fine with it.&#8221;</p></blockquote>
<p><img src="https://www.venganza.org/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/marcusColander.jpg" alt="marcusColander" width="620" height="349" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6459" srcset="https://www.venganza.org/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/marcusColander.jpg 620w, https://www.venganza.org/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/marcusColander-300x169.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 620px) 100vw, 620px" /></p>
<p>Australians are interesting &#8212; the people are clearly enlightened and have a sense of humor but, at the same time, we hear stories of bureaucratic resistance to Pastafarianism coming from Australia more than just about anywhere. I&#8217;ve heard of several denied attempts at getting an Australian ID card colander&#8217;ed photo.  So I was extra pleased to hear that Marcus had pulled it off.  Here&#8217;s the full <a href="http://www.theage.com.au/victoria/this-guy-got-a-victorian-drivers-licence-with-a-pasta-strainer-on-his-head-20161129-gszuu7.html">article in TheAge</a> about how it went down. </p>
<p>&#8230;</p>
<p>Every small victory adds to the weight of legitimacy.  Each wedding officiated by an <a href="https://www.venganza.org/ordination/">FSM Minister</a> and each ID photo taken while wearing Pastafarian headgear (and so on) is an implicit acknowledgement by a government worker that Pastafarianism is a bonafide religious organization.  Over time, I imagine there will be less resistance to what we&#8217;re doing &#8212; it&#8217;ll become clear that we&#8217;re not mocking anyone&#8217;s beliefs, only fighting for our own idea of what religion is about, and making a stand against religious inequality. </p>
<p>Also, on the topic of Australia &#8212; I want to give props to the <a href="https://pastafarians.org.au/">Australia Church of The Flying Spaghetti Monster</a>.  This group is incredibly well organized and engaged in promoting Pastafarianism as a legitimate religion &#8212; and they are doing amazing things in ongoing court battles.  I would urge anyone in the area to check them out and get involved.</p>
