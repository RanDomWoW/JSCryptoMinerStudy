<h1 class="title">Org mode for Emacs &#x2013; Your Life in Plain Text</h1>
<div id="top"><p><em>Org mode is for keeping notes, maintaining TODO lists, planning
projects, and authoring documents with a fast and effective plain-text system.<br />

<span id="top2">Created by <a target="new" href="https://staff.science.uva.nl/~dominik/">Carsten Dominik</a> in 2003, maintained by <a target="new" href="http://bzg.fr">Bastien Guerry</a> and developed by <a href="https://orgmode.org/org.html#History-and-Acknowledgments">many others</a>.</span></em></p></div>

<div id="outline-container-org40820f7" class="outline-2">
<h2 id="intro"><a id="org40820f7"></a>Installation</h2>
<div class="outline-text-2" id="text-intro">

<div class="figure">
<p><img src="img/main.jpg" alt="main.jpg" id="main-image" />
</p>
</div>

<p>
The stable version of Org is <b>9.1.7</b>, as of Feb 2018.  See the <a href="Changes.html">release notes</a>.
</p>

<p>
Get it with <code>M-x package-install RET org RET</code> (see <a href="https://orgmode.org/elpa.html">Org ELPA</a>).
</p>

<p>
Or download it as a <a href="https://orgmode.org/org-9.1.7.tar.gz">tar.gz</a> or <a href="https://orgmode.org/org-9.1.7.zip">zip</a> archives.
</p>

<p>
You can browse the development version from <a href="https://code.orgmode.org/bzg/org-mode">code.orgmode.org</a> or clone the repo:
</p>

<p>
<code>~$ git clone https://code.orgmode.org/bzg/org-mode.git</code>
</p>

<p>
Daily snapshots of the development version: <a href="https://orgmode.org/org-latest.tar.gz">tar.gz</a> or <a href="https://orgmode.org/org-latest.zip">zip</a>
</p>

</ul>

</p>

<div id="outline-container-orgf3c05fe" class="outline-2">
<h2 id="docs"><a id="orgf3c05fe"></a>Documentation and literature</h2>
<div class="outline-text-2" id="text-docs">

<div class="figure">
<p><img src="img/org-mode-7-network-theory.jpg" alt="org-mode-7-network-theory.jpg" width="300px" style="float:right;" />
</p>
</div>

<p>
Buy the <a href="http://www.network-theory.co.uk/org/manual/">printed version</a> of Org's manual (<a href="http://www.network-theory.co.uk/">Network Theory Ltd</a>.)
</p>

<p>
For each book sold, <a href="http://www.network-theory.co.uk/">Network-Theory Ltd.</a> is making a $1 donation to the
maintainer of Org mode.
</p>

<p>
Browse the <a href="https://orgmode.org/manual/index.html">manual</a> (one page per section).
</p>

<p>
Read the complete manual: <a href="https://orgmode.org/org.html">HTML</a> or <a href="https://orgmode.org/org.pdf">PDF</a>
</p>

<p>
Read the compact guide: <a href="https://orgmode.org/guide/">HTML</a> or <a href="https://orgmode.org/orgguide.pdf">PDF</a>
</p>

<p>
Check the <a href="https://orgmode.org/worg/org-faq.html">FAQ</a>, the <a href="https://orgmode.org/worg/org-hacks.html">hacks</a>, and <a href="https://orgmode.org/worg/org-tutorials/">tutorials</a> on <a href="https://orgmode.org/worg/">Worg</a>.
</p>

<p>
Check the reference <a href="https://orgmode.org/worg/doc.html">docs</a> about hooks, commands and options.
</p>

<p>
Check the list of <a href="https://orgmode.org/worg/org-papers.html">(scientific) papers</a> about Org mode.
</p>

<p>
Before submitting a bug report, make sure you read the <a href="https://orgmode.org/org.html#Feedback">"Feedback"</a> section
of the manual.
</p>

<p>
You may also read <a href="http://www.catb.org/esr/faqs/smart-questions.html">How To Ask Questions The Smart Way</a>, by Eric S. Raymond
and Rick Moen.
</p>

<p>
<code>M-x org-submit-bug-report RET</code>
</p>
