            <div style='line-height:1.9em;margin-bottom:20px;'>
                    Probability is based on observations of certain events. Probability of an event is the ratio of the number of observations of the event to the total&nbsp;numbers of the observations. An experiment is a situation involving chance or probability that leads to results called outcomes. An outcome is the result of a single trial of an experiment. The probability of an event is the measure of the chance that the event will occur as a result of an experiment.<br><br><span class="content_important">Probability of an event A is symbolized by P(A). Probability of an event A is  lies between&nbsp; 0 ≤ P(A) ≤ 1.</span>            </div>                     <div class="subtitlehold">
                        <h2  style='font-family: open sans; font-size: 26px;'>    Probability Formula                        </h2>
                        <!-- <a class="backtotop" href="#top" name="what-is-percentage?">Back to Top</a>  <br style="clear:both"/> -->
                    </div>  Probability is the measure of how likely an event is. And an event is one or more outcomes of an experiment. Probability formula is the ratio of number of favorable outcomes to the total number of possible outcomes.<br><b><br></b><span class="content_highlight"><b><br>Probability Formula</b><br><br>Probability of an Event =&nbsp; <span class="content_latex">$\frac{Number\ of &nbsp; Favorable\ Outcomes}{Total\ Number\ of\ Possible\ Outcomes}$</span></span><br>Measures the likelihood of an event in the following way:<br><br>&nbsp;&nbsp; - If P(A) &gt; P(B) then event A is more likely to occur than event B.<br>&nbsp;&nbsp; - If P(A) = P(B) then events A and B are equally likely to occur. <br>                    <div class="subtitlehold">
                        <h2  style='font-family: open sans; font-size: 26px;'>    What is the Formula for Probability                        </h2>
                        <!-- <a class="backtotop" href="#top" name="what-is-percentage?">Back to Top</a>  <br style="clear:both"/> -->
                    </div>  <b>The probability of an event tells that how likely the event will happen.</b> Situations in which each outcome is equally likely, then we can find the probability using probability formula. Probability is a chance of prediction.&nbsp;If the probability that an event will occur is "x", then the 
probability that the event will not occur is "1 - x". If the probability that one event will
 occur is "a" and the independent probability that another event will 
occur is "b", then the probability that both events will occur is "ab".<br><b>Probability of an event&nbsp; A </b>can be written as:<br><br>P(A) =&nbsp;<span class="content_latex">$\frac{Number\ of&nbsp;&nbsp; favorable\ outcomes}{Total\ number\ of\ possible\ outcomes}$</span><br>                    <div class="subtitlehold">
                        <h2  style='font-family: open sans; font-size: 26px;'>    How to Solve Probability Problems                        </h2>
                        <!-- <a class="backtotop" href="#top" name="what-is-percentage?">Back to Top</a>  <br style="clear:both"/> -->
                    </div>  A probability is determined from an experiment, which is any activity that has an observable outcome <b></b>like tossing a coin and observing whether it lands heads up or tails up. The possible outcomes of an experiment are called sample space of the experiment. <b><br></b><span class="content_highlight"><b><br>Steps to find the probability:</b><br><br><b>Step 1: </b>List the outcomes of the experiment.<br><br><b>Step 2: </b>Count the number of possible outcomes of the experiment.<br><br><b>Step 3: </b>Count the number of favorable outcomes.<br><br><b>Step 4:</b> Use the probability formula.<br></span><br>                        <div  class="questionBox">  										<div class="subtitlehold">
										<h2  style='font-family: open sans; font-size: 26px;'> 
										Solved Examples									</h2>
									</div>
									                                <div class="question">
                                    <b>Question&nbsp;1: </b>Two dice are rolled once. Calculate the probability that the sum of the numbers on the two dice is 5.<br/>                                            <b> Solution: </b>
                                            <div class="solution"> Possible outcomes (Sample Space)  = {(1, 1), (1, 2),...............,(1, 
6), (2, 1), (2, 2),................,(2, 6), (3, 1), (3, 
2),...........,(3, 6), .............,(4, 1),  (4, 2),..........,(4, 6), 
(5, 1), (5,2),...............,(5, 6), (6, 1), (6, 
2),......................,(6, 6)}<br><br>Total possible outcomes = <b>36<br></b><br>Number of outcomes of the experiment that are favorable to the event that a sum of two events is 6<br><br>=&gt; Favorable outcomes are: (1, 5), (2, 4), (3, 3), (4, 2) and (5, 1)<br><br>Number of favorable outcomes&nbsp;=<b> 5</b><br><br>Use, probability formula  = <span class="content_latex">$\frac{Number\ of&nbsp;&nbsp; favorable\ outcomes}{Total\ number\ of\ possible\ outcomes}$</span><br><br>= <span class="content_latex">$\frac{5}{36}$<br></span><br>The probability of a sum of 6 is<span class="content_latex"> $\frac{5}{36}$</span>.<br><br/>&nbsp;<br/><br/>                                            </div>                                 </div>                                  <div class="question">
                                    <b>Question&nbsp;2: </b>What is the probability of getting head when tossing a coin.<br><br/>                                            <b> Solution: </b>
                                            <div class="solution"> Sample Space = {H, T}<br>
<br>
Number of possible outcomes&nbsp;= 2<br><br>Number of favorable outcomes&nbsp;= 1<br>(because of only one head) <br><br>=&gt; Probability of getting head  = <span class="content_latex">$\frac{Number\ of&nbsp;&nbsp; favorable\ outcomes}{Total\ number\ of\ possible\ outcomes}$</span><br><br>=<span class="content_latex"> $\frac{1}{2}$<br></span><br>=&gt; Probability of getting head is <span class="content_latex"> $\frac{1}{2}$.<br>
</span><br/>&nbsp;<br/><br/>                                            </div>                                 </div>                          </div>         </div>
