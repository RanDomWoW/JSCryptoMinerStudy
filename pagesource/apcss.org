				</script><h2>Welcome to DKI APCSS</h2>
<p>The Daniel K. Inouye Asia-Pacific Center for Security Studies (DKI APCSS) is a U.S. Department of Defense institute that officially opened Sept. 4, 1995, in Honolulu, Hawaii. The Center addresses regional and global security issues, inviting military and civilian representatives of the United States and Asia-Pacific nations to its comprehensive program of executive education and workshops, both in Hawaii and throughout the Asia-Pacific region.</p>
<p>The Center supports the <a title="U. S. Pacific Command" href="http://www.pacom.mil" target="_blank">U.S. Pacific Command</a> by developing and sustaining relationships among security practitioners and national security establishments throughout the region.  DKI APCSS educates, connects, and empowers security practitioners to build partner capacity, shared understanding, and networks to enhance stability in the Indo-Asia-Pacific region.  It is one of the Department of Defense’s five regional security studies centers. <a href="/about-2/">Read More</a> or go to our <a href="http://apcss.flywheelsites.com/about-2/faqs">Frequently Asked Questions</a> page.</p>
