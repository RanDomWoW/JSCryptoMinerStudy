    <h1 class="mainred">2018 sustainability bill passes<br />
      out of pension commission
    </h1>
    <p> The  Legislative Commission on Pensions and Retirement (LCPR) on Tues., March 13,  passed the 2018 omnibus pension bill. The bill now will be reviewed by various House and Senate committees. <br />
      <br />
The bill  includes sustainability measures for all four public pension systems: the  Teachers Retirement Association (TRA), the Public Employees Retirement  Association (PERA), the Minnesota State Retirement System (MSRS), and the St.  Paul Teachers Retirement Fund Association (SPTRFA). </p>
    <p>Read more <a href="images/pdf/Capitol TRAcker-031418 (2).pdf">HERE.</a></p>
    <p><em>Complete details on the bill, currently moving as SF 2620  / HF 3353, may be viewed on the State Legislature website: <a href="https://www.revisor.mn.gov/bills/text.php?version=latest&amp;session=ls90&amp;number=SF2620&amp;session_year=2018&amp;session_number=0">https://www.revisor.mn.gov/bills/<br />
      text.php?version=latest&amp;session=ls90&amp;number=<br />
    SF2620&amp;session_year=2018&amp;session_number=0</a> </em></p>
    <h1 class="mainred">Commission explores public pension privatization</h1>
    <p>An  organization that spends millions of dollars on a state-by-state effort to  privatize public pensions is now playing a significant role in pension  discussions at the Minnesota state capitol. <br />
      <br />
      The  Legislative Commission on Pensions and Retirement (LCPR) heard testimony on  Jan. 24 from a University of Minnesota research fellow whose pension policy  research is funded in part by the anti-pension Laura and John Arnold  Foundation. Also testifying was the executive director of the Oklahoma Public  Employees Retirement System, whose state&rsquo;s transition away from pensions to a  private, defined-contribution system was partly due to a push from the Arnold  Foundation.<br />
  <br />
    The Feb. 19  LCPR meeting is set to include testimony from a representative of the Pew  Charitable Trust. Pew has received $9.7 million from the Arnold Foundation to support its Public Sector Retirement Systems project  (<a href="http://www.pewtrusts.org/en/projects/public-sector-retirement-systems">http://www.pewtrusts.org/en/projects/public-sector-retirement-systems</a>), according to Governing magazine (<a href="http://www.governing.com/topics/mgmt/gov-john-arnold-pensions.html">http://www.governing.com/topics/mgmt/gov-john-arnold-pensions.html</a>). </p>
    <p>(<a href="images/pdf/Capitol TRAcker-012518.pdf">Read more</a>)</p>
<h1 class="mainred">2018 Federal tax update</h1>
    <p>The 2018 federal tax tables have been entered into the TRA computer system.</p>
    <p>Annuitants with federal tax withholding designations   will notice the change to your net payment amounts starting on February  1.&nbsp; In most, if not all cases, the new tax tables  result in an  <u>increase</u> to your net payment.</p>
    <p>TRA will not send updated annual increase letters.</p>
    <h1 class="mainred">1099-R statements available online</h1>
    <p>The annuitant 1099-R forms for 2017 can now be    printed   through your <em>MyTRA</em> Online account.</p>
    <p>Don't have an account? Watch our <a href="https://www.minnesotatra.org/videos/videos.html#two">video</a> on how to create one.</p>
    <p>A paper copy is scheduled to arrive in your mailbox by January 31.</p>
