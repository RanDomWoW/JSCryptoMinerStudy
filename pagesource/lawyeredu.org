        <h2>Education and Examinations  Steps Necessary To Become a Lawyer/Attorney</h2>
        <p>Today more than ever before, lawyers are needed in a variety of specialties that have arisen due to recent changes in technology, foreign and domestic policy, and health care. More and more lawyers are opting to receive specialized training to become immigration lawyers, intellectual property specialists, health care lawyers, and labor and employment specialists. The U.S. Department of Labor&rsquo;s Bureau of Labor Statistics projects that job opportunities for lawyers across the United States will increase by 10 percent by the year 2020. Although the median annual pay for lawyers nationwide in 2010 was $112,760, this comfortable salary does not come easily. Becoming a lawyer in any state means years of <a href="undergraduate-law-schools.html">undergraduate</a> and <a href="aba-accredited-schools.html">graduate education</a>, as well as passing some of the most difficult examinations required of any profession. </p>
        <p>If you are interested in pursuing a career as a Lawyer or attorney, choose what education level that best describes you: </p>
