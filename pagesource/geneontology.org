<div class="panel-heading">
<h3 class="panel-title">Ontology</h3>
</div>
<div class="panel-body bg-info">
<p>
<a href="http://amigo.geneontology.org/amigo/search/ontology">Filter classes</a>
</p>
<!-- <p> --><!-- <a href="http://amigo1.geneontology.org/cgi-bin/amigo/browse.cgi">Browse class tree</a> --><!-- </p> --><p>
<a href="http://geneontology.org/page/download-ontology">Download ontology</a>
</p>
<p>
Gene Ontology: the framework for the model of biology.  The GO defines concepts/classes used to describe gene function, and relationships between these concepts.  It classifies functions along three aspects:
</p>
<p></p><dl><dt>molecular function</dt>
<dd>molecular activities of gene products</dd>
<dt>cellular component</dt>
<dd>where gene products are active</dd>
<dt>biological process</dt>
<dd>pathways and larger processes made up of the activities of multiple gene products.</dd>

<p>
<a href="http://geneontology.org/page/ontology-documentation">more</a>
</p>
</dl></div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="col-md-6">
<div class="panel panel-default">
<div class="panel-heading">
<h3 class="panel-title">Annotations</h3>
</div>
<div class="panel-body">
<p>
<a href="http://geneontology.org/page/download-annotations">Download annotations</a> <small>(standard files)</small>
</p>
<p>
<a href="http://amigo.geneontology.org/amigo/search/annotation">Filter and download</a> <small>(customizable files &lt;100k lines)</small>
</p>
<p>
    GO annotations: the model of biology.  Annotations are statements describing the functions of specific genes, using concepts in the Gene Ontology.  The simplest and most common annotation links one gene to one function, e.g. FZD4 + Wnt signaling pathway.  Each statement is based on a specified piece of evidence. <a href="http://geneontology.org/page/go-annotations">more</a>
    </p>
