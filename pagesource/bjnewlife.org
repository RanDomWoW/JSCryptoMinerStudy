              and thereby receive the <a href="./english/bstudy/terms_13.php">remission 
              of sin</a> into their hearts and become <a href="./english/bstudy/terms_17.php">righteous</a>.&quot; 
              In other words, it means being born from above. This is not a change 
              that comes from human beings, but it is a transformation that comes 
              from God.</font></p>
            <p><font face="Arial" size="2">In 1 John 5:6-7, the Bible says, &quot;This 
              is He who came by water and blood-Jesus Christ; not only by water, 
              but by water and blood. And it is the Spirit who bears witness, 
              because the Spirit is truth. For there are three that bear witness 
              in heaven: the Father, the Word, and the Holy Spirit; and these 
              three are one.&quot; Jesus came to this earth by water and blood. 
              Jesus was born of the body of the Virgin Mary incarnated into the 
              flesh of a man, and when He turned 30, He was baptized by <a href="./english/bstudy/faq_11.php">John 
              the Baptist</a> in the <a href="./english/bstudy/terms_16.php">Jordan 
              River</a>. The works of salvation that make us be born again of 
              water and the Spirit began with the birth of Jesus, and as He washed 
              away the sins of the world by receiving His baptism from John the 
              Baptist, the <a href="./english/bstudy/terms_09.php">representative 
              of humanity</a>, in the Jordan River.</font></p>
            <p><font face="Arial" size="2">We know very well that Jesus was vicariously 
              condemned in our place by shedding His blood on the <a href="./english/bstudy/faq_10.php">Cross</a>. 
              But why did Jesus, God Himself who was sinless, have to bear this 
              condemnation on the <a href="./english/bstudy/faq_10.php">Cross</a>? 
              There are causes and effects in all things. That Jesus died on the 
              Cross for our sins is very closely related to the event of His baptism, 
              when He was baptized by John the Baptist in the <a href="./english/bstudy/terms_16.php">Jordan 
              River</a>, which was a form of <a href="./english/bstudy/terms_06.php">the 
              laying on of hands</a>. The Apostle Peter said in 1 Peter 3:21 that 
              baptism is an antitype that saves us. This then means that Jesus 
              came by baptism and the Cross.</font></p>
            <p><font face="Arial" size="2">In the <a href="./english/bstudy/terms_07.php">baptism 
              of Jesus</a> is hidden the mystery of the <a href="./english/bstudy/terms_13.php">remission 
              of sin</a>, the mystery of <a href="./english/bstudy/terms_12.php">being 
              born</a> of water and the Spirit. If, in spite of this, we were 
              to ignore this <a href="./english/bstudy/terms_07.php">baptism of 
              Jesus</a> and do not believe in it, then we ourselves would be betraying 
              the will of God and forsaking our own <a href="./english/bstudy/terms_11.php">salvation</a>. 
              The free Christian books of The New Life Mission provide a clear 
              explanation of the standard of salvation by grounding it in thorough 
              and solid Biblical foundations, and they address the basic concepts 
              of salvation as to how one can be born again of water and the Spirit. 
              For more details, we encourage our visitors to read the free Christian 
              books that we provide in printed or electronic format.
